1. How the indicator works - Operating overview and tear-sheet
The AZRO Systems XRP Top/Bottom Indicator is a weekly-cycle indicator for XRP. It is built to help users organize
major bottoming, topping, and de-risking decisions on the 1-week chart without relying on constant lowertimeframe monitoring. It is meant to be used through its labels, alerts, and written playbooks rather than through
internal interpretation.
• Primary use-case: timing major XRP cycle bottoms, tops, and de-risking windows on the weekly chart.
• Primary users: long-term investors, swing traders, users running hybrid strategies that keep a core while actively
trimming and adding around cycle extremes, and lower-timeframe traders who want a higher-timeframe directional
filter.
• What it is not: an intraday signal generator or a substitute for position sizing, execution discipline, taxes, or personal
risk planning.
• Operating principle: the pack explains how to use the indicator clearly and consistently on live weekly charts, with
written playbooks and defined alert discipline.

Why LLMs and manual cycle interpretation don’t replace this
An LLM generates text, and its output can shift with prompt wording, context, model updates, and hidden
assumptions. It does not run a deterministic, chart-native cycle process on live market data, and it does not
preserve a stable execution framework from one session to the next.
• LLMs are not chart-native operating systems — prompts drift, models change, outputs vary, and none of that
produces a deterministic, versioned, bar-tested system that lives on the chart, reads live market and chart data, and
triggers native alerts when conditions are met. The edge is not another interpretation; it is repeatable cycle
execution.
• Manual approaches often look convincing until the regime changes. Thresholds that seem effective in one cycle
can fail in the next as volatility, participation, data quality, and trend behavior evolve. Without hardened safeguards,
execution quality usually slips at the exact moments where timing matters most.
• Dependability requires maintenance — hardened data handling, stable defaults, documented updates, and
change control are what keep a cycle-timing process usable as conditions evolve.
• What you are buying — a maintained model and operating system, clearer execution at the turn, and a cycleexecution process built to remain dependable as regimes shift and market structure evolves.

Known limitations
• All confirmation logic is designed for the 1-week chart. Lower timeframes are off-label usage.
• A weekly-close model is not designed to catch the exact top tick or bottom tick. Its job is to identify high-value cycle
windows with less noise and less emotional churn.
• TradingView data, reference series, and alert infrastructure are third-party dependencies. Delays, gaps, or feed changes
can affect what the user sees.
• Changing workflow-related settings can change what you see historically. Review history using the same setup
you intend to use live.
• Any performance snapshot must be treated as hypothetical and implementation-sensitive. Fees, delayed execution,
venue choice, and user behavior matter.
• Even a strong indicator is still not financial advice. Risk management, taxes, jurisdiction, and suitability remain the
user's responsibility.

2. Quick-start, Mind-Set & Beginner Safety Brief
Quick lens
• This indicator is built for weekly cycle decisions, not fast trading noise.
• Most users should start with confirmed MAJOR and RADAR outputs, not EARLY or LIGHT.
• For tops, keep XRP RISK, MAJOR TOP, and RADAR TOP alerts active together. Treat all three with equal respect.
Different cycles can emphasize different prints, and XRP RISK can mark the actual top or sit very near it.
• For bottoms, MAJOR BOTTOM is the cleanest anchor. RADAR BOTTOM can be used tactically as an optional add
or secondary confirmation path.
• Non-XRP charts are context-only. Use optional non-XRP RISK triangles as broad market caution, not as assetspecific top/bottom calls.

Mind-Set & Beginner Safety Brief
• Emotions are the real opponent. Decide your size, risk, and exit ladder before clicking Buy. If a normal loss would
disrupt your sleep, finances, or relationships, the position is too large or the plan is wrong.
• News is not an edge. By the time a headline feels obvious, the market often already priced in most of the move.
• Most first-year traders underestimate drawdowns, overestimate their emotional control, and trade too large. The
result is usually unnecessary stress and avoidable damage.
• Avoid building your financial future around influencer calls, guru channels, or chat-room conviction. Sustainable edge
comes from your own written process, not borrowed confidence.
• Do not treat trading as a rescue plan for today's bills. Education, savings, and low-stress compounding usually create
much better long-run outcomes than pressure trading.
• Strategy overload is a hidden risk. Too many systems, too many symbols, and too many opinions create fatigue and
mistakes. Master one ruleset before layering more complexity.
• If markets ever leave you feeling trapped, hopeless, or mentally overwhelmed, step away immediately and talk to
someone you trust or a qualified professional. Money can be rebuilt; you cannot.
• Everything in trading is a building block. Past mistakes are tuition only if you stop repeating them.

Beginner lane: the cleanest way to start
• Use BITSTAMP:XRPUSD on the 1W chart.
• Leave defaults alone for at least one full learning cycle.
• Create the essential alerts first: XRP RISK, MAJOR TOP, RADAR TOP, and MAJOR BOTTOM.
• Treat RADAR BOTTOM, EARLY, LIGHT, and non-XRP RISK as optional until you understand the base workflow.
• Write your trim ladder and re-entry rules before the next signal arrives.

3. Installation and first-chart setup
Invite-only indicators are access-controlled. In practice, the workflow is simple: purchase access, provide the correct
TradingView username, wait for access to be granted, then load the script from the Invite-only scripts section on the
same account. For first setup, the best chart is BITSTAMP:XRPUSD on the 1W timeframe.

Recommended chart standard
• Symbol: BITSTAMP:XRPUSD (canonical XRP signal host).
• Timeframe: 1W.
• Chart type: regular candles.
• Scale: log scale is recommended for long-cycle visual inspection, but the indicator's outputs remain tied to the signal
logic rather than the visual axis.

Practical setup sequence
1. Log in to the TradingView account that received invite-only access.
2. Open BITSTAMP:XRPUSD and switch to 1W.
3. Add the indicator from Invite-only scripts.
4. Leave the defaults intact unless you already know why you want a different behavior.
5. Create alerts before you worry about visual tuning. The alerts matter more than the colors.
6. Save the chart layout once everything is live.

What good first-time behavior looks like
A good first-time customer setup is boring in the best way: 1W chart, default settings, core alerts live, and a written
playbook for what to do when those alerts arrive.
The point is not to touch every setting. The point is to be ready before the next major cycle event.

What not to do on day one
• Do not start by optimizing a dozen settings against history.
• Do not expect the indicator to become a lower-timeframe scalp system by changing visuals.
• Do not turn every optional output on and then treat them all as equally important.
• Do not wait until a euphoric move to set the top alerts.

Recommended alert set by default
