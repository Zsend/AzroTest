# 90-day launch plan

## Objective

Prove that Justice Grows can move from verified need to real paid work—and make the evidence public without exploiting candidates.

## Founding 50 × 2

Recruit 50 employers willing to provide at least two real paid roles each. Logos and pledges do not count. Public credit begins only after verification.

## Days 1–30: build the operating core

- Form the operating entity and lived-experience advisory function
- Retain employment, privacy/publication, cannabis-regulatory, and security counsel
- Deploy the secure production data plane in staging
- Train two registry reviewers and two candidate navigators
- Recruit 10 design-partner employers and require named hiring owners
- Build 25 private candidate Passports through trusted reentry and legal partners
- Start California, federal, and Idaho pathway research
- Establish correction, incident, right-of-reply, and story-rights procedures

**Gate:** no real public profile, employer badge, job, claim finding, or registry name before the relevant proof workflow passes.

## Days 31–60: controlled pilot

- Verify employer policies and first paid roles
- Match candidates by skills, location, compensation, and lawful pathway
- Require candidate approval before every introduction
- Track interview, offer, start, wage, benefits, and support needs
- Run first registry cases privately and measure reviewer disagreement
- Test claim review and company response workflow with neutral language
- Publish aggregate progress only where privacy thresholds are met

**Gate:** public beta requires operational correction and profile-hide service levels to be met for four consecutive weeks.

## Days 61–90: public proof launch

- Publish the first verified paid roles
- Publish opt-in candidate talent profiles
- Publish the employer proof ledger and proof funnel
- Publish completed neutral claim findings where warranted
- Publish a small number of fully verified registry records only after legal review
- Launch the Founding 50 × 2 campaign publicly
- Issue a monthly proof report: applications, verified employers, roles, introductions, interviews, offers, starts, retention, advancement, corrections, and coverage gaps

## Employer acquisition

Prioritize operators with real near-term demand:

- Ancillary logistics, packaging, equipment, facilities, construction, software, finance, and professional services
- Multi-state operators with executive-level ownership of fair-chance hiring
- Licensed operators in jurisdictions where role-specific eligibility has been reviewed
- Remote employers and relocation partners for restrictive jurisdictions
- Unions, apprenticeship programs, staffing partners, and workforce boards

The pitch is not “hire a felon.” It is:

> Access capable, motivated talent through a controlled, skills-first process—and earn public credit only for outcomes you can prove.

## Candidate acquisition

Use trusted channels, not broad data scraping:

- Reentry organizations
- Public defenders and post-conviction counsel
- Prison education programs
- Families and directly affected advocates
- Workforce boards and unions
- Candidate self-referrals

Candidate success is measured by quality and mobility, not story volume.

## First-year scorecard

Targets remain separate from actuals:

- 50 verified employers
- 100 verified paid roles
- 500 candidate intakes
- 350 completed Passports
- 200 interviews
- 125 offers
- 100 candidate-confirmed starts
- 75 retained at 180 days
- 30 raises, promotions, credentials, equity, or ownership events
- 250 verified registry records, subject to source availability and safety review

## Board-level metrics

- Median days from candidate intake to first qualified match
- Match-to-interview, interview-to-offer, and offer-to-start conversion
- Starting compensation versus candidate floor
- 90/180/365-day retention
- Advancement and ownership rate
- Candidate satisfaction and complaint rate
- Employer response and evidence completion rate
- Correction volume, severity, and resolution time
- Registry reviewer disagreement and source freshness
- Percentage of public credit supported by candidate confirmation
