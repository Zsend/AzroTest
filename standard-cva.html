Proof Vapor Standard

The standard asks whether a device / oil / material / heating system can be understood under defined lifecycle conditions. It is not a public standard, legal certification, health claim, or regulator-approved method.

Five evaluation gates

1

Material Path Truth

Identify and map oil-contact and vapor-contact materials, components, unknowns, and claims.

2

Static Oil Contact / Storage Stability

Evaluate what happens after filled systems sit over time and under defined storage conditions.

3

Aerosol After Storage

Evaluate what comes out after the system has aged, been partially used, stored, heated, or stressed.

4

Thermal Control / Failure Modes

Assess chain-pull, near-empty, hotspot, heater-aging, and use-state behavior under controlled procedures.

5

Full-System CVA Readiness

Decide whether the record is complete enough for a CVA-style structured evidence artifact.

Record, not claim

CVA — Certificate of Vapor Analysis

CVA is a structured evidence record tied to a tested system and defined limitations. It can reference device version, standard version, test matrix version, status, public/private fields, corrections, expiration, and revocation.

ArtifactWhat it can sayWhat it cannot say
