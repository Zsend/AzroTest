What this benchmark means

The benchmark is a comparison framework. It is designed to keep AZRO accountable against a defined weekly standard, not to promise future results.

A good benchmark page should make the process easier to judge over time. It should preserve version context, weekly records, limitations, and revisions.

This page is designed to show

  • the current public benchmark entry;
  • the version active for that entry;
  • the weekly proof cadence;
  • public-safe notes and limitations;
  • links to the longer proof archive.

This page is not designed to show

  • proprietary model logic;
  • internal thresholds;
  • signal formulas;
  • raw proof files;
  • exact trigger rules;
  • investment advice.
