Label glossary (XRP)

What each label means, and how it’s intended to fit into a weekly-close workflow.

Core confirmations (weekly close)
Use these as workflow anchors — not predictions.
MAJOR Confirmation

Major confirmation. This is a primary “cycle-level” signal evaluated on the weekly close.

RADAR Confirmation

Radar confirmation. A “heads up” style confirmation designed to highlight developing conditions.

LIGHT Confirmation

Light confirmation. Used to highlight important-but-lighter confirmation conditions in the workflow.

EARLY Confirmation

Early confirmation. Intended to surface earlier-stage conditions for tracking and preparation.

Risk triangles Risk

Risk markers intended to highlight elevated risk conditions. Use them to tighten risk management and re-check your plan.

Optional context overlays
These do not trigger confirmations. They provide additional context.
Market Context Suite Optional

Optional overlays designed to provide market context while keeping the chart readable.

1W RSI Zones Optional

Configurable RSI zones designed to highlight overheating/cooling in a weekly-close workflow.

Alert setup
You can create TradingView alerts for confirmations so you don’t need to monitor charts all day. See the Alert Trigger Guide for recommended configurations.

BTC Engine glossary

A quick map of the dashboard language.

Weekly action (BUY / HOLD / TRIM)

The BTC Engine outputs a weekly action based on its regime logic. It’s designed to support a consistent process, not to predict price.

Suggested amount

A sizing suggestion derived from the selected mode + risk profile. Treat it as a framework to calibrate your plan, not a mandate.

Modes (Stack / Adaptive / Event / Performance)

Execution modes control how buys scale and how trims behave. Modes are built to match different risk tolerances and objectives.

BTC-eq (proof vs auto-buy)

A comparative metric that simulates how a rules-based plan would have performed against simple auto-buy behavior over the selected period.
