Frequently asked questions

Expand an item for a concise response.

What is AZRO, in one sentence?
A rules-based Bitcoin treasury program that helps a business allocate a sustainable portion of surplus cash flow into BTC with governance, reporting, and operational controls.
Is this investment advice?
No. The materials are educational and process-oriented. You should consult your financial, legal, and tax advisors before implementing any treasury policy.
Do I need to start big?
No. Many businesses begin with a small fixed contribution (e.g., $20/week) and only scale if it remains operationally safe through volatility.
Is this a trading strategy?
The program is designed for accumulation and discipline. Optional trims can be used to manage liquidity and concentration, but the intent is not frequent trading.
What makes this different from “just buying BTC”?
Most of the value is operational: written policy, execution rules, buffers, custody decisions, reporting cadence, and stakeholder alignment—so the program survives cycles.
How do you handle volatility?
Volatility is addressed through sustainable sizing, cash buffers, no leverage, and rules that prevent reactive decision-making.
What about custody and key management?
Custody is a core design choice. Options include self-custody with strong operational security, multi‑sig setups, or institutional solutions—depending on your balance sheet and expertise.
How do taxes factor in?
Taxes can materially affect outcomes and vary by jurisdiction and entity structure. Treat tax planning as part of the operating system and align with your advisors.
Can I pause or stop the program?
Yes. A healthy policy includes clear conditions for pausing contributions (e.g., cash flow stress) and for resuming once the business is stable.
Why focus on Bitcoin rather than “crypto” broadly?
Bitcoin’s core characteristics—decentralization and issuerlessness—make it meaningfully different from many other digital assets with different governance and risk profiles.
