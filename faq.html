FAQ & concerns

Answer the real questions with calm, professional clarity.

Concerns aren’t noise. They’re due diligence. A treasury program is stronger when the hard questions are addressed directly, with time‑horizon framing and operational detail.

Common concerns

A concise set of answers you can use in real conversations.

“Quantum computing breaks it.” Reality: not immediate
Quantum risk is not an emergency upgrade cycle. The prudent stance is: monitor, upgrade only when necessary, and follow broad consensus. If cryptography standards evolve, the ecosystem can migrate with coordinated upgrades—similar to other security transitions on the internet.
“Power grid / internet outages.” Reality: all systems pause
If connectivity fails broadly, commerce pauses. A decentralized network resumes when connectivity returns. The more important operational question for a business is contingency planning: cash management, continuity, and access controls.
“Backed by nothing.” Reality: backed by properties
It’s backed by its properties: scarcity, durability, decentralization, and proof‑of‑work security. Think of it as digital property with globally verifiable ownership.
“Energy use / ESG backlash.” Reality: energy is security
Energy is the cost of incorruptible settlement security. Mining tends to move toward the lowest‑cost energy—often stranded or curtailed. In some contexts it can also support grid economics by acting as a flexible buyer of last resort.
“Volatility.” Reality: horizon + governance matter
A treasury program should assume volatility and operate with policy, limits, and a long time horizon. That is precisely why the program focuses on repeatable allocation and unit accumulation rather than short‑term prediction.
“Regulatory risk.” Reality: focus on compliance
The direction of travel has been toward greater institutional adoption and clearer rules in many jurisdictions. Your program should be structured to comply with your local tax, accounting, and reporting requirements.
“Custody risk.” Reality: operational controls
Custody is solvable with procedures: role separation, approvals, secure key storage, and (where appropriate) multi‑sig or qualified custody. The program includes governance templates so custody is a process, not a person.
“Competition from other tokens.” Reality: different category
A treasury program focuses on the digital commodity asset class: issuerless, decentralized, and globally verifiable. Other projects may be platforms or venture‑style assets, but they are structurally different categories.
