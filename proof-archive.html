What archive entries include

Approved public archive entries may include:

  • proof week and date;
  • product and version;
  • public-safe benchmark note;
  • broad workflow state, if approved;
  • founder note;
  • revision status;
  • limitations and disclosures;
  • link back to the current benchmark page.

Every public entry should be clear enough to build trust and constrained enough to protect the system.

What archive entries do not reveal

Archive entries do not reveal Pine source, thresholds, formulas, trigger logic, exact timing recipes, raw chart exports, internal proof files, customer/private material, or internal research.

Revision policy

Archive entries may be annotated, corrected, revised, or superseded. AZRO should preserve the revision history rather than quietly rewriting the record.

A proof archive is only useful if the history remains visible.

Version policy

Every archive entry should identify the product version active at the time of the entry. Version context matters because public records should not blur old systems, current systems, and future systems.

Current BTC proof entries should reference BTC Operating System v1.1.1 unless a newer source-truth release supersedes it. XRP should remain secondary/optional unless explicitly included in a founder-approved proof entry.
