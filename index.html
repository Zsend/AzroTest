The system

What Reserve Standard is.

A Bitcoin treasury operating system for businesses. Five components, working together — built and maintained so the discipline compounds across decades instead of breaking under the first stress test.

01

Live operating model.

Published on TradingView and runnable in the business's own account. Every week: one clear action and one clear amount — a treasury allocation, not a trade.

02

Written policy framework.

Cash separated by job. Reserve allocation, custody path, approval logic, and review cadence — defensible at audit and easy for a partner, CFO, accountant, or successor to inspect. The record matters because memory is not governance.

03

Weekly cadence.

Open the model. Read the amount. Execute the buy. Send to cold storage. Log it. Move on. Five to ten minutes a week, so treasury never takes over operations.

04

Team Reserve.

The same discipline extended to employees through education and personal application. Stage one stays clean: no employer match, no payroll deduction, and no employer-executed purchases.

05

Review and maintenance.

Quarterly inspection of policy adherence, treasury position, and process drift. Changes are documented, so the system stays useful across market cycles instead of going stale. Preserve the center. Adapt the edges.

The order is the strategy

Capitalize the company first. Then capitalize personally.

The owner who only builds personally ends up with a stronger wallet and a weaker business. A weak business eventually stops paying. If the foundation gives way, the wallet stops getting filled — no matter how disciplined the buying was. Strengthen the foundation first, and the personal position ends up larger, longer, and on a base that can sustain it.

Tax efficiency

The dollars buy more Bitcoin.

Pay yourself first and a business dollar becomes roughly $0.50–$0.70 of personal cash after tax. Holding Bitcoin on the balance sheet preserves the full dollar at the point of accumulation. Over a long horizon, that gap compounds enormously.

Balance-sheet strength

Optionality the wallet can't give you.

A business with Bitcoin reserves has structural advantages personal BTC cannot provide: creditworthiness, acquisition optionality, stronger valuation, collateral, and the operational firepower to act when competitors are forced to sell.

Continuity

The reserve outlives any single owner.

Company-held Bitcoin stays with the business through partner buyouts, succession, and sale. Personal Bitcoin doesn't transfer. The mission is stored business energy — and the business that earned the dollars deserves the same monetary protection the owner gives themselves.
