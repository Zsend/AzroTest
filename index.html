Movement launch · Idaho first

A tablet should be a bridge, not a paywall.

In the founding Idaho case behind this movement, people described spending days, months, or years in locked rooms without sunlight, fresh air, or even access to a book — while a correctional tablet sat in the room and still offered no real free path to learn, heal, or prepare for release.

That is not just inefficient. It is degrading. And it is far more solvable than most people realize.

The case

This is what wasted human time can look like from the inside.

A person can be caged with a device that is good enough to carry paid content, good enough to mediate contact, and good enough to be monetized — but still not treated as a place where they have a real free right to learn, regulate, or prepare for release.

Days. Months. Years.

People can lose enormous stretches of life in county jails and prison units where waiting is treated as normal and growth is treated as optional.

No sunlight or fresh air

In the founding Idaho case and in letters from inside, people describe long stretches without the basic conditions most outsiders would assume still exist.

Not even a book

In the founding case, books could not simply be sent in. The result was isolation without meaningful educational alternatives.

Paid fragments instead of a path

For many people inside, charging for broken or stripped-down learning is functionally the same as denying access.

Founder note

The founding picture is not complicated.

Shared rooms. Long waits. Men deteriorating. Tablets in reach. No real free path to a GED. No daily structure built to protect mental health. No continuity that could carry into prison or release. That gap is what A Right For All is trying to close.

“The device was already there. The human need was already there. The missing piece was a meaningful free floor.”

The solution

The first fix is simple. The impact is not.

This movement is not starting by asking for open internet, perfect institutions, or a total rebuild of corrections. It is starting with a clear free baseline on hardware that already exists.

Not open internet

The ask is not unrestricted web access. It is a meaningful, secure free path to learning and basic wellness on existing devices.

Not a luxury request

GED access, daily regulation tools, and reentry preparation are not extras when a person’s future is already being shaped by confinement.

Not the whole finish line

The free minimum is the floor. It does not replace the need for deeper education, counseling, programming, and human support.

Not technically out of reach

Once the rail already exists, the central barrier becomes will, approvals, incentives, and public pressure — not pure technical impossibility.

Free full GED prep

A real path to foundational learning on the devices people already have — not a symbolic checkbox and not a paywall.

Basic daily wellness

Simple tools for regulation, reflection, stress reduction, and forward motion — because education does not stick when people are trapped in survival mode.

A free section that actually teaches

The free side of the tablet should help a person better themselves. It should not stop at the bare minimum of what institutions are already forced to provide.

Continuity across moves

Progress should survive transfers, holds, prison placement, and release instead of forcing people to start over from zero every time the system moves them.

Make the free section teach. That is the first public standard. It is simple enough to understand and strong enough to organize around.

Why now

This is a live infrastructure problem, not a fantasy problem.

The point is not that nothing exists. The point is that the baseline is still too weak where the time loss is most brutal. Official sources already show the rail is real.

Idaho prisons now issue free tablets

IDOC says each resident has been provided a free Bridge 8 tablet. Once the hardware is already in the room, the argument shifts from “impossible” to “why is the free floor still this weak?”

Official source ↗

Jerome County Jail already runs on ViaPath / GettingOut

The founding county-jail context is not technologically disconnected from the correctional tablet ecosystem. The vendor rail is already part of the environment.

Official source ↗

Jail time is not trivial time

The Bureau of Justice Statistics says people admitted to local jails spent an average of 32 days in custody before release in 2023. That average hides much longer stretches like the ones described in the founding case and letters.

BJS source ↗

Khan’s public mission already points toward the contradiction

Khan Academy says its mission is to provide a free, world-class education for anyone, anywhere, and publicly lists correctional facilities as a use case. That makes paid or degraded access inside an especially sharp moral pressure point.

Once the hardware is already there, the question is no longer whether something better is technically possible. The question becomes whether we are willing to demand it.

The bigger build

GED + basic wellness is the floor, not the ceiling.

The larger product vision is an intelligent daily workbook designed for the people most likely to be ignored by traditional software, education systems, and reentry planning.

Check in

Understand stress, mindset, goals, and immediate needs without making the person feel reduced to a case file.

Stabilize

Offer breathing, reflection, exercise, and practical mental-wellness tools that help a person get out of raw survival mode.

Learn

Guide a person through GED and foundational learning paths that actually fit the setting, the time horizon, and the real barriers inside.

Plan

Support future orientation, trade exploration, and next-step thinking instead of letting incarceration become a total suspension of the future.

Continue

Carry progress through jail, prison, and reentry instead of forcing the person to restart every time the system changes their location.

The vision is not just “more content.” It is a humane daily layer that helps a person stabilize, learn, and carry progress forward.
