Hard questions

Trust must be engineered.

Are you publishing a list of “felons” for employers?
No. The public registry and private career network are intentionally separate. Employers cannot browse candidates by conviction. Career profiles begin with skills, preferences, and consent.
What does “nonviolent cannabis” mean?
It is not a personality label. The registry evaluates the current commitment and publishes a dated evidence statement, such as: “No violent offense identified in the current commitment records reviewed as of [date].” Material non-cannabis counts remain visible to reviewers.
Will every cannabis employer be able to hire every candidate?
No. State licensing rules, local rules, job duties, supervision terms, transportation, federal restrictions, and employer-specific obligations can matter. The platform uses a state-and-role eligibility workflow and human review rather than automatic rejection.
Can an employer call itself fair-chance without hiring anyone?
It can only be “Pledged.” “Verified” requires policy and role review. “Proven” requires confirmed starts and retention outcomes. Public status can be suspended after complaints or noncompliance.
How does candidate privacy work?
Personally identifiable intake data is encrypted. Consent is specific, revocable, and logged. Candidate information is not sold. Public storytelling is never required for services or placement.
Is this legal advice or a background-check service?
No. State-access information is operational guidance that must be reviewed by qualified counsel before adverse or licensing decisions. Justice Grows is designed not to provide employers a conviction-screening marketplace or automated adverse-decision tool.

Accuracy is a standing obligation.

Report an identity, custody, source, talent-profile, job, privacy, or metric issue. Corrections enter a private evidence queue and never change public data automatically.
