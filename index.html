The reserve problem

Most businesses already run a reserve policy. It is just not written down.

Once the month is covered, excess cash still needs a job. Treating every dollar the same protects access, but weakens reserve.

01

Operating cash

Keeps payroll, rent, vendors, taxes, and working capital moving.

02

Safety cash

Buys the company time when revenue softens or something breaks.

03

Opportunity cash

Stays ready for obvious high-conviction moves inside the business.

04

Reserve cash

Should carry stored business energy forward instead of slowly weakening.

The separation that matters: operating cash is about access. Reserve cash is about long-run strength.
Why Bitcoin fits reserve

The reserve job needs scarcity, direct ownership, and digital liquidity.

Cash handles access. Reserve capital needs a form built for long-duration strength.

  • Cash is necessary for operations, but weak for long-duration reserve.
  • Equities and funds add valuation, management, and dilution risk.
  • Real estate and gold preserve scarcity, but add friction and custody burden.
  • Bitcoin combines fixed supply, direct ownership, portability, and global liquidity.

Cash / money market

Access

Right for near-term obligations. Weak for true reserve once the short-term job is done.

Equities / funds

Exposure

Useful in other contexts, but still paper claims with management and market risk layered in.

Real estate / gold

Scarcity

Closer to reserve logic, but slower to move, verify, divide, custody, and settle.

Bitcoin

Reserve fit

Direct digital property with fixed supply, portability, liquidity, and lower impairment for the reserve job.
