Human potential is not disposable.

Build the minimum humane layer.

People can spend days, months, or years in custody while a digital device already sits in the room. A Right For All is building the standard and infrastructure for free learning, basic stabilizing support, and human-development access on correctional tablets — with special urgency for neurodivergent and unusually capable people the system is most likely to misread or lose.

The first demand is simple: free GED prep and basic wellness on every correctional tablet. The long-term mission is bigger: make sure human potential is not wasted just because a person is locked away, stigmatized, or hard for normal systems to understand.

The operating thesis

The rail is already in the room. The baseline is not.

  • The device can carry learning, regulation, and preparation.
  • The baseline should be free before anything is sold.
  • The standard should survive vendors, facilities, politics, and time.

A Right For All is not asking society to pity people in custody. It is asking society to stop wasting human potential beside technology that already exists. The first standard is free GED prep and basic wellness. The deeper mission is to find, protect, and develop people the system is most likely to discard.

The minimum humane layer

Start with a baseline everyone can understand.

A movement can be huge and still begin with one practical standard. If a tablet is already in the room, the free section should teach, stabilize, and prepare a person for release.

1

Free learning

Real GED prep and foundational learning access, built for the correctional tablet environment and free to the person using it.

2

Basic stabilizing support

Daily wellness and regulation tools that help people stay oriented, set goals, and keep their minds from being wasted by confinement.

3

Neurodivergent-aware design

Support for people whose attention, sensory processing, executive function, or unusual cognition can be punished by systems that do not understand them.

This is not soft. Education, structure, regulation, and opportunity are public-safety infrastructure. A person who can learn, stabilize, and build a plan is different from a person left to decay.

Inside the room

Picture the actual room, not the policy brochure.

This movement starts from an actual contradiction: a locked room, a mind under pressure, years of human time at stake, and a device that can already carry something better.

Founding Idaho testimony

The founding case described people spending days, months, or years in confinement without sunlight, fresh air, or books, while tablets were already present and shared inside the room.

Time

Days become months. Months can become years.

Waiting becomes normal. Growth becomes optional. Human time gets burned instead of redirected.

Conditions

No sunlight. No fresh air. Not even a book.

The letters from inside describe a setting where people are expected to survive without the basic tools that help a mind stay human.

Contradiction

The device is there. The free path may not be.

Communication, entertainment, and payment can reach the room while serious free learning and stabilizing support remain absent or too weak.

The people most likely to be lost

Some minds need the right environment or they break in the wrong one.

A Right For All is also building for neurodivergent, high-potential, and unusually capable people who may be punished, isolated, or misunderstood by systems that cannot read their strengths. The mission is not to label people. It is to build supports that let unusual minds become contribution instead of collapse.

Design for nervous systems under pressure

Confinement, uncertainty, sensory stress, isolation, and loss of agency can push vulnerable people toward shutdown, rage, despair, or self-destruction. Basic regulation support should not be treated as optional.

Find potential before it disappears

Some people who look impossible in standard systems may have rare pattern recognition, intensity, creativity, memory, technical ability, or problem-solving gifts. The challenge is to build a route that sees usefulness before life collapses.

Build for those inside and outside

If the tablet route succeeds, it scales inside. If it takes longer, the same mission still helps people coming out, families, advocates, and neurodivergent builders outside the walls.

For professionals: how we will handle this responsibly

We will not turn neurodivergence into a slogan, diagnosis filter, or excuse. This work needs clinicians, educators, researchers, accessibility thinkers, people with lived experience, and corrections operators to help define what support is safe, evidence-informed, and practical.

The standard

The first standard is narrow on purpose.

The long-term institution can become large. The first standard must be simple enough to repeat, test, and require.

What the device already proves

The rail exists.

  • Tablets are already present in correctional environments.
  • They already shape attention and communication.
  • They already support paid or controlled services.

What should be guaranteed

A free human floor.

  • Free GED preparation.
  • Basic wellness and regulation tools.
  • Continuity across custody and reentry.
  • Neurodivergent-aware accessibility and design.
