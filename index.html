Movement launch · Idaho first

A tablet should be a bridge, not a paywall.

People in jail and prison already sit with correctional tablets in their hands. Those devices should offer a real free path to GED prep, basic wellness, and reentry — not just a thin free section and paid fragments of self-improvement.

We are starting with a simple public demand: free GED prep and basic wellness on every correctional tablet.

This is the kind of device already in people’s hands. The infrastructure is no longer the excuse.

The public line is simple.

This is not about pretending nothing exists. It is about forcing a real free baseline where the rail already exists.

The rail already exists. The device is already there. The time is already there. The human need is already there. The missing piece is a meaningful free floor.

Why this matters

The system already uses tablets. It still wastes human time at scale.

In too many places, people spend days, months, or longer holding devices that could support real progress — while the actual free experience remains too weak, too fragmented, or too easy to ignore. That is a policy failure, a design failure, and a moral failure.

Idle time becomes harm

People can sit for long stretches with almost no meaningful structure to help them learn, regulate, or prepare for release.

Families pay twice

When learning is paywalled, people inside choose between calling home and trying to better themselves.

County jail is the blind spot

Before prison, during holds, and between court dates, people can lose enormous stretches of life in environments built for custody — not growth.

The public demand

Start with the free minimum.

This movement does not need to begin with a perfect institution or a billion-dollar system. It begins with a baseline every facility can be pushed toward.

Free full GED prep

A real path to foundational learning on the devices people already have — not a symbolic checkbox and not a paywall.

Basic daily wellness

Simple tools for regulation, reflection, stress reduction, and forward motion — because education does not stick when people are in survival mode.

A real free section

The free side of the tablet should teach. It should not stop at entertainment, religion, or law resources.

Continuity over custody gaps

The learning path should not reset every time a person moves between jail, prison, and release.

The bigger vision

The endgame is an intelligent daily workbook built for the people who need it most.

The first public demand is the floor. The product vision goes further: a humane, secure, daily layer that helps a person stabilize, learn, set goals, and carry progress forward.

Check in

Understand stress, mindset, goals, and immediate needs.

Stabilize

Offer breathing, reflection, exercise, and practical mental wellness tools.

Learn

Guide a person through GED and foundational learning paths that actually fit the setting.

Plan

Help with future orientation, trade exploration, and next-step thinking.

Continue

Carry progress through jail, prison, and reentry instead of forcing people to restart from zero.

This is not a prison reform slogan. It is a practical minimum-rights standard with a larger product path behind it.

Real strategy

A real movement needs a real operating path.

The goal is not to look large before we are real. The goal is to build enough signal, evidence, and leverage that institutions have to take the minimum standard seriously.

1

Build proof

Collect stories, letters, founder testimony, and facility-level evidence that make the problem visible without overclaiming.

2

Define the standard

Turn outrage into a clear baseline: what free GED and basic wellness should actually mean on a correctional tablet.

3

Earn a pilot path

Find the fastest credible route through a facility, vendor, partner, or platform that can move from concept to proof.

4

Make it durable

Use proof to drive procurement language, policy pressure, and broader adoption instead of relying on one good actor.

FAQ

What this is — and what it is not.

Are you saying no education exists in corrections today?

No. The point is that access is still too uneven, too facility-dependent, and too weak on the devices people already have. The movement is aimed at the free baseline, not at erasing work that already exists.

Why start with GED and wellness?

Because it is concrete, morally legible, and scalable. It is the strongest first floor for a broader system that can later include deeper education, recovery, and reentry tools.

Why movement first instead of building the full app immediately?

Because the bottleneck is not just code. It is legitimacy, access, vendor reality, approvals, and public pressure. A movement creates leverage the product can later use.

Why Idaho first?

Because the founding case is real there, because the founder lived this problem there, and because Idaho is a sharp enough proving ground to make the case visible and concrete.
