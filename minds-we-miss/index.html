Designed for everyone

Built to reach the people systems miss.

Some people are punished before their needs, differences, or abilities are understood. This is not a separate cause. It is a design principle for the whole mission—and eventually an upstream prevention effort.

See the three horizonsHelp shape the work
The rule

No diagnosis is required to deserve the baseline.

No diagnosis excuses harm. Different minds still need systems capable of teaching, communicating, and supporting change.

Why this belongs here

The justice system magnifies every weakness in how society understands different minds.

Differences connected to attention, sensory processing, communication, executive function, learning, brain injury, trauma, or overload can be misread as laziness, defiance, manipulation, aggression, or refusal.

Under-identified

People may enter custody without diagnosis, accommodations, or language for what they experience.

Misinterpreted

Shutdown, overwhelm, impulsivity, blunt communication, or difficulty following complex instructions can be treated only as misconduct.

Unsupported under pressure

Isolation, uncertainty, sensory stress, disrupted care, and loss of agency can remove protective factors when people need them most.

Before, inside, and after

Change the full trajectory—not only the moment of release.

Before

Recognize needs and ability earlier.

Long term, work with families, schools, youth programs, diversion, juvenile justice, mentors, and specialists so avoidable crisis does not become a permanent justice-system path.

Inside

Build tools that work under pressure.

Clear language, predictable structure, accessible learning, regulation support, and constructive challenge for different ways of thinking.

After

Turn potential into a real path.

Connect people to education, work, mentors, care, community, and environments where their abilities can become contribution instead of collapse.

Universal frame

People arrive through many doors.

The baseline belongs to everyone.

Support cannot depend on innocence, exceptional talent, a diagnosis, political popularity, or whether the public finds someone easy to relate to.

Design for the hardest-to-reach user.

A system that works for people with low literacy, sensory stress, executive-function challenges, trauma, or unusual cognition will work better for everyone.

Some minds are punished before they are understood. Some potential is lost before it is ever recognized. We are building a system that sees more—and wastes less.
Potential under pressure

Difference can become contribution—or collapse.

A Right For All does not romanticize neurodivergence. It refuses to assume that a person who struggles in a conventional system has nothing valuable to contribute.

Build predictability

Clear language, routines, visible structure, and low-friction navigation.

Build regulation support

Grounding, planning, sensory awareness, emotional vocabulary, and daily structure.

Build useful challenge

A constructive place for curiosity, intensity, pattern recognition, creativity, memory, and deep focus.

Build continuity

Support that continues through release, work, housing, treatment, relationships, and community.

Evidence with restraint

The risk is serious. The claims must stay honest.

Research points to elevated vulnerability among neurodivergent people and people leaving custody. The exact overlap is still under-studied. A Right For All will not invent one dramatic multiplier or pretend the evidence is more settled than it is.

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger or experiencing suicidal thoughts in the United States, call or text 988.

What we will do

  • Link qualified research and inspection findings
  • Separate established evidence from open questions
  • Work with clinicians, researchers, and people with lived experience
  • Build support without diagnosing people through software
The Minds We Miss agenda

Research. Design. Continuity. Talent.

Research

Connect fields that often remain separate: neurodiversity, corrections, education, suicide prevention, and reentry.

Design

Build accessible learning and regulation tools with qualified experts and users.

Continuity

Support the transition into work, care, housing, family, and community.

Talent

Create proof-over-pedigree paths for unusual thinkers without requiring diagnosis disclosure.
