For employers

Do not hire from guilt. Hire with better information.

A background screen can identify history. It cannot measure every skill, judgment, work habit, or future contribution.

We help you see more—without lowering standards or ignoring law.

The ask

Bring one real paid opportunity.

Not a broad pledge. Not an awareness campaign. A role, project, apprenticeship, interview, or business mentorship with clear terms and a real decision-maker.

01

State the work

What must be done, what can be learned, and what success looks like.

02

State the pay

Wage range, project fee, hours, benefits, location, and timing.

03

State the restrictions

Separate genuine law, licensing, insurance, or safety requirements from habit and assumption.

04

Review the person

Consider current evidence and role fit individually where the law allows.

The value

The candidate still earns the role. You make a better-informed decision.

Prohibition Careers does not certify moral character or promise performance. We organize relevant evidence, clarify constraints, and support a responsible evaluation.

Better signal

See work, references, readiness, and fit—not only a background result.

Lower-friction evaluation

Use a structured interview, bounded paid project, apprenticeship, or direct hire.

Post-start visibility

Track retention, pay, responsibility, and repeat hiring instead of counting promises.

When law blocks the door

Employer willingness cannot override licensing law.

Some jurisdictions limit or prohibit direct cannabis-industry work based on conviction history. Those restrictions must be identified honestly.

When direct placement is not lawful, opportunity may still exist through cannabis-adjacent technology, design, packaging, logistics, professional services, remote work, mentorship, procurement, entrepreneurship, and policy reform.

Future employer standard

Open Industry will be earned by action.

No badge for signing a pledge. Recognition follows verified paid opportunities, interviews, hires, retention, advancement, and repeat participation.
