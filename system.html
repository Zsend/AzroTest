System architecture

Designed around one clean job.

CragLink Power is not trying to be every power station. The system is built around Starlink Mini use at remote basecamp: a right-sized battery, solar recovery, vehicle top-up path, and a stable DC output.

BatteryTarget: 12V 100Ah LiFePO4. Enough reserve for real overnight use.
SolarTarget: 300W folding panel. Recovery during daylight at camp.
VehicleReady for an isolated DC-DC charging path when driving.
OutputTarget: stable 48V DC output for Starlink Mini compatibility.

Design constraints

Real constraints beat extra features.

The product should stay narrow until customer demand proves expansion. The first job is Starlink Mini power, not a generic inverter box.

Primary useRemote basecamp internet power for Starlink Mini.
Power philosophyDC-native architecture to reduce unnecessary conversion and confusion.
PortabilityCar-camping portable: modular battery box + separate folding panel.
ChargingSolar first, vehicle top-up path second, AC charger optional.
Safety postureFuses, protected branches, polarity control, and clear instructions should be part of the kit.
Brand promiseThe cheapest reliable complete system for running Starlink Mini all day at basecamp.
