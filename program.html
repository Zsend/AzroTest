Program

A treasury program you can run while operating a business.

The objective is simple: convert a fixed slice of free cash‑flow into long‑horizon purchasing‑power optionality, governed by policy and measured in units accumulated.

Low overhead cadence
Policy‑driven execution
Monthly reporting

Principles

The program is built to reflect the asset’s monetary properties and the reality of running a small business.

Units first

We measure the treasury in units accumulated over time. Price matters, but unit growth is what compounds optionality.

Rules over emotion

Execution is policy‑driven. The goal is to reduce “decision fatigue” and improve consistency across cycles.

No leverage mandate

The program is designed to avoid the fragile path. A durable treasury should not require leverage to work.

How it works

A practical sequence—built to fit your calendar.

1) Set a treasury policy

Define allocation rate, custody approach, spending constraints, and who can approve changes. This is the “constitution” of your program.

2) Establish an operating buffer

The business runs on cash. A buffer allows treasury activity to be systematic instead of reactive.

3) Run the allocation cadence

Allocate a fixed slice of cash‑flow on a schedule. The program’s edge comes from discipline + repeatability.

4) Report monthly, improve quarterly

Monthly: units accumulated, cost summary, and policy compliance. Quarterly: review limits, custody, and operations.

What makes this different from “an LLM plan”
An LLM can summarize ideas. It cannot give your business a governed operating system, a reporting standard, an execution template, and a repeatable cadence that can survive stress without drift.
