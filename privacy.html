Trust standard

Privacy by architecture

Candidate-controlled career mobility requires more than a privacy statement. It requires structural separation between public evidence, private employment data, and employer access.

Operating draft · Counsel review required before collecting real sensitive data
Core rule

Justice Grows does not sell candidate data, publish private contact information, or expose people as a searchable conviction marketplace.

What the platform collects

Candidate intake may collect contact information, location at the state or city level, work experience, skills, career goals, compensation preferences, support needs, and consent choices. Registry referrals may contain identity, custody, court, and source information necessary for private evidence review. Employer intake collects organization, role, policy, and contact details.

Separation of systems

Public evidence

Only reviewed registry fields, source metadata, confidence, freshness, and correction history.

Private mobility

Contact details, support needs, exact circumstances, and employer introductions remain access-controlled.

Voluntary profiles

Public talent profiles require separate candidate choice and moderation. They show capability and ambition—not conviction details.

Aggregates

Small cohorts are suppressed to reduce re-identification risk. Unavailable data is never presented as zero.

Candidate control

  • Private by default.
  • Separate choices for private matching, coalition-only visibility, and public visibility.
  • A distinct approval before any employer introduction.
  • Immediate public-profile hiding and a correction, export, withdrawal, and deletion channel subject to lawful retention.

Production security baseline

Real deployment requires encryption at rest and in transit, named administrator accounts, MFA, least privilege, row-level security, audit logging, tested backups, incident response, dependency review, access reviews, retention schedules, and periodic penetration testing. GitHub Pages local test mode is not approved for real personal information.

Prohibited uses

No housing, credit, insurance, immigration enforcement, debt collection, surveillance, harassment, adverse employment screening, bulk scraping, or re-identification. The platform is an opportunity and accountability system—not a people-search product.
