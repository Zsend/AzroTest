Trust standard

Privacy and story rights

Career mobility requires structural separation between public evidence, private employment data, and employer access—not a checkbox buried in a privacy statement.

Public beta standard · Real-data launch requires the secure data plane
Your story belongs to you

Applying, interviewing, accepting work, or receiving services never grants marketing rights. Public use of a candidate’s identity, image, voice, story, or conviction history requires separate informed consent, a defined use, a time limit, and agreed fair compensation.

Core rules

  • Private by default.
  • No candidate fees.
  • No sale or rental of candidate data.
  • No searchable conviction marketplace.
  • No employer access before a candidate-approved introduction.
  • No pay-to-rank employer badges or sponsored proof levels.

Separation of systems

Public evidence

Only reviewed registry fields, public employer proof, source metadata, confidence, freshness, and correction history.

Private mobility

Contact details, support needs, exact circumstances, compensation preferences, and introductions remain access-controlled.

Voluntary profiles

Public talent profiles require separate candidate choice and moderation. They show capability and ambition—not conviction details.

Aggregates

Small cohorts are suppressed to reduce re-identification risk. Unavailable data is never presented as zero.

What the platform may collect

Candidate intake may include contact information, location at the state or city level, work, skills, goals, compensation preferences, support needs, and granular consent choices. Registry referrals may contain identity, custody, court, and source information necessary for private evidence review. Employer intake may contain organization, role, policy, contact, and outcome information.

Candidate control

  • Separate choices for private, coalition-only, and public visibility.
  • A distinct approval for every employer introduction.
  • Immediate public-profile hiding.
  • Access-key rotation.
  • Correction, export, withdrawal, and deletion channels subject to lawful retention.
  • Storytelling interest is not permission to publish.

Production security baseline

The GitHub Pages build stores fictional acceptance-test data in one browser and is not approved for real personal information. Real deployment requires a separate secure API and database, encryption at rest and in transit, named accounts, MFA, least privilege, row-level access controls, audit logging, tested backups, incident response, retention schedules, vendor review, periodic access reviews, and independent security testing.

Prohibited uses

No housing, credit, insurance, immigration enforcement, debt collection, surveillance, harassment, blacklisting, adverse employment screening, bulk scraping, re-identification, or coercive media use. Justice Grows is an opportunity and accountability system—not a people-search product.
