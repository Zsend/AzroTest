Mission brief

Start with the real constraint.

A Right For All begins with a new baseline: wherever correctional tablets are used, the free section should include real GED preparation, basic wellness, accessible design, and preparation for release.

Start with the right

Make the Free Floor a required baseline through law, policy, purchasing, and contracts.

Build The Bridge

Keep learning, goals, and support alive from county jail to prison to home.

Change the trajectory

Build better paths for neurodivergent and overlooked people before, inside, and after justice involvement.

Current wedge: Idaho first. Evidence first. The smallest useful workbook before a giant finished platform. The user-facing baseline stays free.

FAQ

How to approach this well.

Do I need a résumé?

No. Proof beats pedigree.

Can I use research or software tools?

Yes. Disclose material tools, sources, or collaborators. We care about judgment, taste, and honesty.

Do I need outside research?

No. The mission brief is enough. Additional research is welcome if disclosed.

Can I use bullets, diagrams, or a deck?

Yes. Use the format that makes your thinking clearest.

Please do not include: confidential legal details, unnecessary medical information, or anything you would not want stored in a normal application process.
