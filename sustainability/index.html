Sustainability

The promise stays free because the model is built around it.

A Right For All will not build a core learning and support system that depends on charging people in custody, charging families, or asking the public to rescue the operating budget every year.

Mission lock

No prisoner or family pays for core access.

The systems around the user fund implementation, standards, support, evaluation, and long-term protection.

Economic logic

Free to the person. Funded by the systems that benefit.

Implementation and support

Agencies, facilities, education partners, and technology providers can fund setup, training, integration, maintenance, and evaluation.

Standards and accountability

Accessibility review, implementation guidance, certification, reporting, and purchasing support can fund the institution around the Free Floor.

Research and long-term protection

Grants can support evidence and expansion; reserves and an eventual endowment can protect the mission through political and budget cycles.

Capital follows proof

Build the control package before the giant institution.

These are planning ranges, not a current fundraising offer. Each stage should earn the next through evidence, relationships, and implementation learning.

$0–$25K

Doctrine, evidence controls, professional review, public site, advisor network.

$25K–$100K

Research, legal and privacy work, prototype, partner exploration, pilot design.

$100K–$500K

First implementation, technical work, content, training, and evaluation.

$500K–$2.5M

Repeatable model, multiple sites, Standard v1.0, implementation team.

Antifragile by design

If one route closes, the mission keeps compounding.

No single vendor

The standard should travel across different technology rails.

No single funder

Institutional revenue, philanthropy, and reserves should diversify support.

No single founder forever

Governance, documentation, succession, and mission lock must preserve the work across generations.

Current boundary: A Right For All is developing its entity, funding, partnership, pilot, and legal posture. The site does not claim nonprofit status, tax deductibility, an approved pilot, facility adoption, vendor commitment, or measured public impact unless those facts are documented.
