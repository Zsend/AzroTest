Sustainability · free-user doctrine

Free to the person inside. Funded by the system around it.

The incarcerated-user layer must stay free. The long-term model funds the infrastructure around that free floor through agencies, vendors, implementation support, certification, training, research, philanthropy, and eventually reserve/endowment capital.

Mission lock

No prisoner or family should pay for the baseline human-development floor.

GED prep and basic wellness are the starting point, not the ceiling.

Institutional payers

Agencies, facilities, vendors, and partners can fund implementation without charging the person who needs access.

Standards and certification

Over time, the free floor can be supported by standards, review, training, implementation help, and certification.

Reserve capital

A 100-year institution needs protected capital so the mission can survive politics, vendor cycles, and donor fatigue.

Current boundary

This is not fundraising copy.
