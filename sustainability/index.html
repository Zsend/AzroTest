Sustainability

The promise stays free because the model is built around it.

A Right For All will not build a baseline that depends forever on charging people in custody, charging families, or asking the public to rescue the operating budget every year.

Mission lock

No prisoner or family pays for core access.

The system around the user funds the standard, implementation, measurement, and long-term protection.

The economic logic

Free to the user. Funded by the systems that benefit.

Implementation and technical assistance

Agencies, facilities, partners, and vendors can fund setup, training, integration, support, and evaluation.

Standards and accountability

Certification, audits, procurement support, accessibility review, and reporting can fund the institution around the free floor.

Research and reserve capital

Grants and philanthropy can fund expansion and evidence; reserves and endowment eventually protect the mission through political and budget cycles.

Capital follows proof

Build the control package before the giant institution.

These ranges are planning estimates, not fundraising offers or legal claims. Each stage should unlock the next through evidence, relationships, and real implementation learning.

$0–$25kDoctrine, evidence controls, professional review, public site, advisor network.
$25k–$100kResearch, legal/privacy work, prototype, partner exploration, pilot design.
$100k–$500kFirst implementation, technical work, content, training, evaluation.
$500k–$2.5MRepeatable model, multiple sites, standard v1.0, implementation team.
Long-duration reserve goal

A permanent institution may eventually require a substantial reserve or endowment—potentially tens of millions of dollars—so the free user layer survives donor cycles, political changes, and budget shocks. That is a long-term mission-protection goal, not a present status claim.

Antifragile by design

If one path closes, the mission keeps compounding.

The app is one route. The larger assets—evidence, standards, professional trust, outside resources, reentry continuity, implementation knowledge, and a cross-partisan coalition—continue to grow.

No single vendor

The standard should work across multiple technology rails.

No single funder

Institutional revenue, philanthropy, and reserve capital should diversify support.

No single founder forever

Governance, documentation, succession, and mission lock must preserve the work across generations.

Current boundary

Structure before claims.

A Right For All is developing its entity, funding, partnership, pilot, and legal posture. The site does not claim nonprofit status, tax deductibility, an approved pilot, a facility commitment, a vendor commitment, or measured public impact unless and until those facts are documented.
