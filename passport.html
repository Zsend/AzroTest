Candidate control

You decide whether your Passport is private, coalition-only, or public. A public profile requires explicit consent and staff moderation.

  • Hide a public profile immediately.
  • Rotate the secure access link.
  • Approve or decline each job match.
  • No candidate fee and no sale of candidate data.

Your story belongs to you

Applying, interviewing, accepting work, or receiving services never grants marketing rights. Public use of your identity, image, story, or conviction history requires separate informed consent, a defined use, a time limit, and agreed fair compensation.

What employers see

Verified employers receive role-relevant skills and match information. Identity is introduced only after candidate consent. Conviction details do not power the match score.

Proof, not promotion

Employer proof levels come from reviewed roles and candidate-confirmed outcomes. Donations and sponsorships cannot alter your match, an employer’s ranking, or the public ledger.

Secure access

Your secret link is equivalent to a password. Rotate it if it was shared, opened on a public device, or included in an email thread you do not control.

Need a correction?

Use the public correction channel for a talent profile, registry record, job, privacy concern, or metric. Evidence is reviewed; public data is never auto-edited.
