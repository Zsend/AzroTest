The larger goal

The count should be known. The goal should be zero.

America does not maintain a reliable current national count of people incarcerated for cannabis. The first rule is simple: do not turn uncertainty into a marketing statistic.

What we know nowUnknown

Federal, state, local, tribal, jail, prison, pretrial, and supervision data are fragmented.

Historical rough estimate≈32,000

A state-and-federal-prison estimate derived from older data. Not current, complete, or a confirmed nonviolent count.

The destination0

No one incarcerated solely for nonviolent cannabis conduct.

An improving count may rise before it falls.

Finding previously invisible cases increases what is known. That is better coverage—not necessarily more incarceration.
