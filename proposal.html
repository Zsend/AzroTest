Proposal

A proposal for a stronger Bitcoin treasury.

For businesses that want to accumulate more long-term Bitcoin from less cash, with a maintained, low-stress weekly system that extends to the people building the company.

The hidden problem

Every business already has a reserve decision.

The moment the month is covered and the business still keeps cash, a decision is being made — even by default. Leaving it in dollars is the decision. The first job of a serious treasury system is to make cash separation explicit, written, and reviewed on a stable cadence.

Treasury logic

The four jobs of business cash.

Operating, safety, opportunity, and long-run reserve cash should not be treated as one undifferentiated pile. Only the last bucket is true long-run reserve. Once the four jobs are separated, the treasury question becomes specific: what belongs in that bucket, and how is the allocation executed week after week?

The asset

Bitcoin is the reserve asset answer.

Gold is scarce by what we can currently access. Bitcoin is scarce by mathematical law. Fixed supply, digital settlement, global liquidity, divisibility, portability, and verification make it the stronger long-run reserve asset for a business operating in a digital world.

The execution problem

The asset can be right and the plan can still fail.

Blind buying creates stress. Reserve decisions drift. Volatility pressures the owner to interfere. Reserve Standard is built to solve the weekly execution problem with a maintained model, written policy, custody path, approval logic, review cadence, and simple operating routine.

Order of operations

Company first. Team next. Owner last.

The business treasury comes first because the company is the vessel that earned the dollars. Once the company system is healthy, the same sound-money logic can extend to the people building it.

Decision point

The proposal should lead to a review, not a public signup.

Reserve Standard is selective. The Bitcoin Treasury Review determines whether the business's cash profile, owner intent, and team context fit the maintained system.
