Free-floor standard · procurement control point

The clause is the control point.

A Right For All is not only asking people to care. The durable route is to make the free floor legible enough that agencies, facilities, vendors, and partners can put it into contracts, policies, and implementation plans.

Core standard

Free GED prep and basic wellness on every correctional tablet.

No user charges. No family charges. No ads. No paywall restrictions for the baseline.

Draft procurement language

Make the free floor explicit.

Draft standard language: Contractor shall provide, maintain, and make available at no cost to incarcerated users a baseline education and wellness access layer on all covered correctional tablets. The baseline layer must include GED preparation resources and basic wellness/regulation tools, and shall not include user charges, family charges, advertisements, paid upgrade prompts, or paywall restrictions for core access.

Free to the person inside

The baseline cannot depend on an incarcerated person or family member paying to unlock it.

Contract-ready

The standard should be clear enough to live inside RFPs, vendor agreements, agency policies, and implementation reviews.

Measurable

Facilities and vendors should be able to show whether the free floor exists, is accessible, and actually works on the tablets in use.

Plain English: if the tablet rail already exists, the humane minimum should not be optional, hidden, degraded, or locked behind another payment.
