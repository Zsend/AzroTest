The free floor should be contractable, measurable, and free to the incarcerated user.

A Right For All is defining a minimum human-development floor for correctional tablets: free GED prep, basic wellness/regulation tools, and correctional-tablet-native access with no user charge, no family charge, no ads, and no paywall inside the baseline.

ARFA Free Floor Standard v0.9

The first standard is intentionally narrow.

The standard is not a full app promise, a national legal claim, or a pilot claim. It is a practical baseline that facilities, vendors, agencies, educators, and funders can pressure-test.

Free GED prep

Correctional-tablet-native GED preparation and study resources available to incarcerated users without charge.

Basic wellness

Nonclinical regulation, grounding, reflection, and goal-setting tools that support daily stability without claiming to replace treatment.

Procurement-ready

A standard that can appear in RFPs, contracts, vendor requirements, implementation checklists, and facility reporting.

Draft procurement control point

The clause is the control point.

Draft standard language: Contractor shall provide, maintain, and make available at no cost to incarcerated users a baseline education and wellness access layer on all covered correctional tablets. The baseline layer must include GED preparation resources and basic wellness/regulation tools, and shall not include user charges, family charges, advertisements, paid upgrade prompts, or paywall restrictions for core access.
