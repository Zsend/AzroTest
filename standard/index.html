The first standard

Easy to understand. Hard to remove.

Every correctional tablet should include a meaningful no-cost baseline for learning, stability, and preparation—not a symbolic link hidden behind paid extras.

See the standardPressure-test it
The public promise

No one should have to buy their way into a chance to change.

The baseline remains free to the person in custody and their family.

Free Floor Standard v0.1

Four parts. One minimum.

1

Free GED preparation

Correctional-tablet-native learning and study resources available without user or family charges.

2

Basic wellness and regulation

Nonclinical grounding, reflection, emotional-regulation, goal-setting, and daily-structure tools.

3

Neurodivergent-aware access

Readable, predictable, low-friction design that accounts for different attention, sensory, communication, and executive-function needs.

4

Continuity into reentry

A way to carry goals, learning progress, and preparation forward when custody ends.

What basic wellness means

Useful support without pretending to replace treatment.

The Free Floor can support grounding, reflection, breathing, routines, sleep hygiene, planning, emotional vocabulary, goal-setting, and knowing when to seek help. It should not diagnose, provide crisis care, or replace clinicians.

What it must avoid

  • Therapy-replacement claims
  • Clinical diagnosis by software
  • Ads, upsells, or paid prompts inside the baseline
  • Data collection that is not necessary for the user benefit
  • Design that punishes low literacy or different cognitive styles
The control point

The clause turns the moral claim into an operating requirement.

Draft procurement language: Contractor shall provide, maintain, and make available at no cost to incarcerated users a baseline education and wellness access layer on all covered correctional tablets. The baseline shall include GED preparation resources and basic wellness/regulation tools and shall not include user charges, family charges, advertisements, paid upgrade prompts, or paywall restrictions for core access.

In the RFP

Make the baseline a requirement before vendor selection.

In the contract

Define availability, accessibility, privacy, reporting, maintenance, and renewal terms.

In public accountability

Measure whether the free floor is present, usable, and genuinely free.

Responsible technology

AI is not the mission. Human development is.

AI may eventually support tutoring, reflection, accessibility, and planning. It must remain subordinate to educators, clinicians, attorneys, corrections judgment, privacy, and human relationships.

Human oversight

AI cannot replace accountable professional judgment.

Privacy by design

Collect the minimum data needed and never turn vulnerable users into a product.

Evidence before deployment

Safety, bias, accessibility, and educational value must be tested rather than assumed.

Help improve the standard

A serious standard is built in public with the right professionals.

Educators, corrections operators, accessibility experts, clinicians, procurement specialists, privacy lawyers, technologists, people with lived experience, and cross-partisan policy professionals can help define what is useful, safe, measurable, and implementable.
