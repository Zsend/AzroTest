Nothing on this site or in related materials is personalized financial, tax, legal, or accounting advice. Businesses should review reserve sizing, custody, reporting, and tax consequences with their own advisors.

Digital asset risk

  • Bitcoin and digital assets can be highly volatile.
  • Markets can gap, reprice, or remain under stress for long periods.
  • Historical illustrations are educational only and do not guarantee future outcomes.

Execution and custody risk

  • Reserve Standard does not custody assets or move funds for clients.
  • Businesses remain responsible for exchange counterparty risk, self-custody risk, institutional custody selection, and internal approvals.
  • Operational mistakes, lost credentials, or weak controls can cause permanent loss.

Policy and governance risk

  • A reserve policy can reduce chaos but cannot eliminate business risk, market risk, or human error.
  • Optional trims, exceptions, or policy changes should be documented and approved internally.
  • Reserve Standard does not guarantee that any workflow will outperform a benchmark or preserve purchasing power.

Bottom line

Use conservative sizing, maintain real operating and safety buffers, avoid leverage, and review the full workflow with your own advisors before relying on it with business funds.
