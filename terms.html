Platform standard

Terms of participation

Justice Grows creates opportunity and public accountability without overstating legal, employment, licensing, release, or impact outcomes.

Public beta operating terms · Counsel review required before real-data launch
No guaranteed outcome

Justice Grows does not promise release, employment, licensing, record relief, relocation, funding, promotion, equity, ownership, or legal results. Information is not legal advice.

Candidate participation

Candidates provide information in good faith and control visibility. Participation is free. A candidate may decline any role, introduction, service, media request, public profile, or outcome confirmation without losing access to ordinary platform services.

Story rights

A job application or service relationship does not grant rights to a candidate’s identity, image, story, voice, or conviction history. Public use requires separate informed consent, a specific scope, a time limit, a revocation process where feasible, and agreed fair compensation. Consent to be contacted about storytelling is not consent to publish.

Employer participation

Employers must submit real, currently funded paid roles; transparent compensation; role-specific lawful review; candidate privacy; individualized fair-chance consideration; outcome reporting; and a credible advancement path. Employers may not demand public storytelling as a condition of hiring, services, promotion, or continued employment.

Proof and sponsorship

A pledge, donation, logo, campaign, or sponsorship does not establish verified status. Sponsorship cannot buy ranking, proof level, positive findings, verification speed, access to private candidates, or softer methodology. Public credit is tied only to reviewed evidence and candidate-confirmed outcomes.

Evidence submissions and right of reply

Public claims submitted for review remain private research leads until evidence review supports a disposition. Material company conclusions require a documented response opportunity. Submitters must not provide privileged, stolen, unlawfully obtained, or needlessly sensitive information.

Prohibited conduct

  • Charging candidates for access or placement.
  • Scraping, bulk downloading, or attempting to identify private participants.
  • Harassment, surveillance, blacklisting, retaliation, or adverse screening.
  • Coercive, uncompensated, or misleading use of candidate stories.
  • Publishing exact release destinations, home addresses, medical information, Social Security numbers, or protected communications.
  • Submitting false roles, false outcomes, or manipulated evidence.

Moderation, correction, and removal

Justice Grows may hold, correct, annotate, suspend, downgrade, or remove records, profiles, employers, roles, claims, and accounts when evidence is incomplete, consent is missing, safety risk is credible, policy is breached, or law requires action. Material proof decisions must be documented and appealable through the correction process.
