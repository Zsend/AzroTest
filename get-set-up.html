Member setup

Get set up.

Four steps, about an hour in total — and we do every one of them with you, screen-share or in person. You stay in control throughout: Reserve Standard never holds, moves, or touches your funds or your Bitcoin.

Step 1 · 10 minutes

See the model.

Create a free account at TradingView.com, then send your TradingView username to support@reservestandard.com. After access is approved, open the published Reserve Standard script page below and add it to your saved weekly BTC chart. If TradingView shows Request Access, confirm that the username matches the one you sent us and continue through Reserve Standard support — not TradingView comments.

Open Reserve Standard on TradingView Create a free TradingView account
Step 2 · ~20 minutes

Open your business buying account.

We use River — a Bitcoin-only financial institution with dedicated business accounts. No altcoins, no trading noise. Regulated and SOC 2 audited, with proof of reserves and FDIC-insured cash balances; business accounts carry no monthly or custody fees, and your business gets a named relationship manager. Connect your business bank account and you are ready to buy. Your money sits in your River account — never with us.

Open a River Business account
Step 3 · ~30 minutes, together

Set up your storage.

Order two things. First, a Trezor Safe 3 — Bitcoin-only edition (about $60): an open-source hardware wallet with a certified secure-element chip, so your Bitcoin lives with you — not on an app, not on an exchange. We set it up together. Second, a SEEDPLATE (about $50, by Coinkite — one of the most respected names in Bitcoin security hardware): a steel backup plate you mark your recovery words into, independently destruction-tested. Store it somewhere safe, separate from the Trezor.

These two pieces of hardware — about $110 in total, paid to the makers, not to us — are the expected out-of-pocket setup hardware cost. And they are yours.

Get the Trezor Safe 3 (Bitcoin-only) Get a SEEDPLATE

One thing we are direct about: self-custody means you hold the keys. Lose both the Trezor and the steel plate, and no one — including us — can recover the Bitcoin. That is exactly why we do this step together, back it up on steel, and welcome your accountant or a second key person in the room.

After this step, your reserve answers to you and no one else.

Step 4 · at your Review

Write your rules.

You set every number — the framework does the heavy lifting. The Review walks you through the four jobs of business cash — operating, safety, opportunity, reserve — so you can see clearly what is genuinely long-run reserve. You decide how much, and you decide the pace. Your decisions then become your written policy: the weekly cadence, the liquidity guardrails, who approves what — written, logged, and reviewable by your accountant.

That split is deliberate. Reserve Standard provides the framework, the tools, and the documentation. The numbers are always yours.

Every week after

The five-minute cadence.

Open the model → read the one action → execute on River → move it to your Trezor on your cadence → log it. That is the whole job. No charts, no guesswork, no daily noise.
