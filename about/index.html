Independent initiative

Built in public. Clear about what it is.

A Right For All is an independent, founder-led public-interest initiative in development. This page explains its current status, how the site handles information, and how the evidence archive is presented.

The current promise

Human potential is not disposable.

The first demand is free GED prep and basic wellness on every correctional tablet. The initiative is building evidence, standards, relationships, and practical paths toward that goal.

Current status

An initiative—not yet a nonprofit or formal public institution.

A Right For All is not currently presented as a nonprofit, tax-exempt organization, government program, correctional-facility program, vendor product, approved pilot, or official partner of any organization unless a specific page explicitly says otherwise.

No donation claims

The site does not accept donations or represent contributions as tax-deductible. A future entity and funding structure may be developed later.

No implied endorsement

References to agencies, facilities, vendors, education providers, or research organizations are documentary or contextual—not claims of endorsement or partnership.

No finished-product claim

The site presents a movement, a proposed Free Floor standard, evidence, and a contributor path. It does not claim a nationally deployed application or approved integration.

Letters and provenance

Evidence with a human source.

Each published letter was personally given to the founder by its writer for use in A Right For All. The writers verbally authorized project use, including advocacy and publication. Typed excerpts are lightly edited only for readability and remain paired with the source documents.

A writer may request a correction, updated attribution, or removal. The founder’s own public letter redacts the founder’s legal name; the unredacted source is preserved privately.

Open the lettersRequest a correction or removal

What may be removed before publication

  • Home addresses, phone numbers, booking numbers, or case identifiers
  • Names of minors or sensitive third-party details unrelated to the testimony
  • Information that creates a specific safety or retaliation concern
  • The founder’s legal name, consistent with the founder’s privacy choice
Privacy and participation

The site collects as little as possible.

Contact form

The help form opens your own email application and sends nothing automatically. Do not include confidential legal details, unnecessary medical information, or sensitive information about another person.

Unusual Thinkers Challenge

Challenge responses autosave only in your browser until you export them. The page does not transmit your answers to A Right For All. Exported packets are submitted manually by email.

No tracking stack

This static site does not include advertising, behavioral analytics, or a third-party application backend in the current build.

No employment promise

Taking the Challenge or contacting the initiative does not promise selection, paid work, employment, partnership, funding, or a response.

Important boundary

Information and advocacy—not medical, legal, or crisis care.

A Right For All does not provide medical diagnosis, therapy, legal representation, crisis response, or correctional-facility services. Research and testimony are presented to support public understanding and responsible development of the mission.

In the United States, people experiencing suicidal thoughts or an immediate mental-health crisis can call or text 988. In an emergency, contact local emergency services.
