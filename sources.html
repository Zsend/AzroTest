Sources & method

Truth before marketing.

Every public number should say what it measures, what it does not measure, and when it was last reviewed.

01

Cannabis incarceration count

Last Prisoner Project explains why the current national count is unknown, why the old 40,000 figure is not precise, and how an extrapolation produced a rough ≈32,000 state-and-federal estimate.

Last Prisoner Project — count explainer
02

Legal cannabis employment base

Vangst and Whitney Economics reported approximately 440,000 full-time-equivalent legal cannabis jobs in early 2024. This is an employment-base estimate, not a count of open positions.

Vangst — 2024 Jobs Report discussion
03

Formerly incarcerated unemployment

Prison Policy Initiative estimated 27.3% unemployment among formerly incarcerated people in 2008 and 31.6% among those released within two years. It remains the only nationally representative comparable estimate cited by the report; it is not cannabis-specific or current.

Prison Policy Initiative — Out of Prison & Out of Work
04

Employer review principles

EEOC guidance discusses job-related screening, the nature and gravity of the conduct, time elapsed, the nature of the job, and individualized assessment in relevant circumstances. The guidance itself states that it does not have the force and effect of law.

EEOC enforcement guidance
05

Federal Bonding Program

The U.S. Department of Labor operates a fidelity-bonding program for eligible workers and employers. Eligibility and availability depend on current program rules.

U.S. Department of Labor — Federal Bonding Program
06

State cannabis licensing restrictions

Rules vary by state and role. Utah is one example of a jurisdiction where agent-card rules can restrict direct work inside a licensed medical-cannabis establishment based on felony history.

Utah Department of Agriculture and Food — agent information

Publication rules

What we will not do.

We will not call ≈32,000 a current verified count.

We will not call ≈440,000 open jobs.

We will not present broader formerly incarcerated unemployment as a cannabis-specific rate.

We will not publish impact numbers until the underlying outcomes are verified.

We will update source dates and explanations when better data becomes available.
