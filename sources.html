Sources & method

Truth before marketing.

Every public number should say what it measures, what it does not measure, and when it was last reviewed.

01

Cannabis incarceration count

Last Prisoner Project explains why the exact current national count is unknown, why 40,000 is not precise, and how a later rough extrapolation produced an incomplete estimate of about 32,000 people in state and federal prisons.

Open source
02

Legal cannabis employment base

A Vangst and Whitney Economics estimate reported 440,445 full-time-equivalent legal cannabis jobs as of March 2024. This is an employment-base estimate, not a vacancy count.

Open report
03

Formerly incarcerated unemployment

Prison Policy Initiative estimated 27.3% unemployment among formerly incarcerated people in 2008 and 31.6% among people released within two years. The figures are broader than cannabis convictions and are not current.

Open source
04

Employer review principles

EEOC guidance discusses job-related screening, time elapsed, the nature of the conduct, the nature of the job, and individualized assessment in relevant circumstances.

Open guidance
05

Federal Bonding Program

The U.S. Department of Labor operates a fidelity-bonding program for eligible workers and employers. Eligibility and availability depend on current program rules.

Open program

Publication rules

What we will not do.

  • Call 40,000 or 32,000 a current verified national count.
  • Call 440,445 open jobs.
  • Present broader formerly incarcerated unemployment as a cannabis-specific rate.
  • Publish impact numbers before the underlying outcomes are verified.
  • Hide the date, scope, or limits of a statistic.
