Public accountability

Proof and evidence methodology

Authority comes from separating claims from outcomes, showing what is known, documenting how it was established, and correcting errors quickly.

Public beta standard · Version 2.0
Core rule

A statement is a signal. A reviewed role is an opportunity. A candidate-confirmed start, retention milestone, raise, promotion, equity grant, or ownership pathway is an outcome. Justice Grows never collapses them into one vague impact number.

Employer proof ladder

1. Policy verified

Compact commitments, named contacts, candidate privacy, story rights, and a role-specific fair-chance process are reviewed.

2. Role verified

A currently funded, paid role passes compensation, duties, pathway, manager, and publication review.

3. Hire verified

A candidate confirms the paid start after a candidate-controlled introduction.

4. Retention verified

A qualifying start reaches at least 180 days through the outcome workflow.

5. Mobility verified

A candidate confirms a raise, credential, promotion, equity grant, or ownership pathway.

Right of reply

Material company conclusions require evidence review and a documented opportunity for the company to respond.

What does not count

A logo, pledge, donation, sponsorship, conference appearance, social post, policy statement, scraped job, press release, interview promise, or employer-reported outcome does not by itself count as a verified role or result. Sponsorship cannot change proof level, ranking, methodology, or review speed.

Candidate confirmation

Introductions require candidate approval. Career starts and mobility events require candidate confirmation before public counting. Employer confirmation may supplement the record but does not replace the candidate’s voice. Small cohorts are suppressed when publication could identify a participant.

Career matching

Matching uses candidate-selected skills, target roles, location, remote and relocation preferences, lawful pathway, schedule, and compensation. Conviction details are intentionally excluded from automated scoring. Licensing and role eligibility receive separate human and legal review before an introduction.

Custody registry standard

Publication sentence

“No violent offense was identified in the current commitment records reviewed as of the stated date.” The platform evaluates records; it does not permanently label a human being.

  1. Resolve identity across source systems.
  2. Confirm current custody or supervision status.
  3. Establish cannabis attribution to the current commitment.
  4. Review all material current counts, enhancements, consecutive terms, detainers, and resentencing orders.
  5. Apply and date the violence-screen statement.
  6. Document source provenance and last-check time.
  7. Obtain two independent approvals with every checklist item complete.

Confidence and freshness

Confirmed

Operative court or commitment records support the material fields.

Supported

Multiple reliable records align, but a primary record remains outstanding.

Provisional

Private research only. Never included in confirmed public totals.

Disputed

Held or annotated while conflicting evidence or a correction is resolved.

Every public record carries a last verification date. Projected release dates are separate from confirmed release events. Missing or inaccessible data is unavailable—not zero.

Corrections and appeals

Credible disputes involving identity, custody, violence screening, release, company proof, candidate consent, or safety trigger preservation of the prior value, review by someone other than the original decision-maker, and temporary field freezing or unpublishing when risk warrants it. Material changes receive a dated note.
