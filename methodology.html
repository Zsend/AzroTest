Performance addendum Research context

Backtest overview

The simulation uses weekly data and a fixed contribution schedule. Strategies are rules-based and do not require prediction. The output is best viewed as an execution sanity check — not a guarantee of future returns.

What’s modeled

  • Regular contributions (weekly)
  • Rules-based position adjustments
  • Simple fee/tax assumptions inside the model outputs
  • Equity curve and drawdown tracking

What’s not modeled (common pitfalls)

  • Market impact from very large orders
  • Exchange outages / custody failures
  • Behavioral deviations (panic selling, overtrading)
  • Jurisdiction-specific tax treatment

Interpretation

We optimize for a process that a real business can run: no leverage, documented controls, and a cadence that survives stress. The goal is durability across cycles — not maximizing backtest charts.

Robustness checks

The same rules can behave differently depending on start date and regime. We therefore look at sensitivity rather than pretending a single window is “the truth.”

Rules and controls (high level)

We keep the product usable and auditable. The details are in the customer packet and policy memo template.

Funding rule

Only allocate from free cash flow on a fixed schedule. No borrowing; no leverage.

Risk rule

Adjust exposure using a rules-based valve approach. The goal is to manage drawdowns while staying in the game.

Governance rule

Document the policy, custody, approvals, and reporting. Treat this as treasury, not entertainment.

AZRO
