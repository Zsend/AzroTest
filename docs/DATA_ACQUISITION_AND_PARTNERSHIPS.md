# Data Acquisition and Partnership System

## Registry acquisition

### Federal

- Known-identifier status checks from the federal locator
- Judgments and sentencing records from federal court systems
- Recurring Bureau of Prisons FOIA extracts for cannabis-linked commitments and release actions
- Attorney, clemency, and advocacy referrals
- Same-day release reconciliation where a known date approaches

### States

For each department of corrections:

1. Obtain a stock file of current people, controlling/commitment offenses, statutes, sentence, facility, projected release, and status.
2. Obtain recurring admissions, releases, resentencing, transfer, death, and status deltas.
3. Map offense descriptions and historical statute versions.
4. Retrieve operative court records for inclusion candidates.
5. Document unavailable fields and agency update cadence.
6. Maintain records-request calendar, appeal log, fees, data dictionary, and source health.

### Counties

Jail data is fragmented and high-churn. Add only where the legal and operational use case justifies the cost, with stronger freshness controls and clear separation from prison counts.

## Data partnerships

Priority partner types:

- Public defenders and post-conviction counsel
- Legal-aid and record-relief providers
- Reentry organizations
- Clemency and cannabis-justice advocates
- Departments of corrections and court administrators
- University criminal-justice and data labs
- Newsrooms with public-record expertise
- Workforce boards, community colleges, unions, and apprenticeship programs

## Employer partnerships

Recruit employers through:

- Cannabis trade associations and social-equity programs
- State and local licensee lists
- Supplier and vendor directories
- Multi-state operator HR/social-impact teams
- Ancillary technology, packaging, equipment, construction, finance, compliance, marketing, and logistics networks
- Unions and staffing firms
- Workforce grants and state equity programs

Do not list an employer publicly based on a press quote. Require a signed compact, policy review, approved role, and outcome reporting.

## Candidate-service network

Every launch geography needs named partners for:

- Identification documents
- Benefits and healthcare navigation
- Housing and transportation
- Digital access and phones
- Work clothing/tools
- Training and credentials
- Legal relief and supervision questions
- Mental health and substance-use support when requested
- Relocation planning
- Financial coaching and credit building
- Retention and workplace mediation

## Records-request template fields

Request machine-readable CSV/Excel where maintained:

- Full name and agency identifier
- Custody status and actual/projected release
- Commitment/controlling offense description and statute
- All current commitment offenses and sentence components
- Admission, sentencing, resentencing, and release actions
- County/court of commitment
- Facility and supervision type, excluding unsafe release-location detail
- Agency violence/classification field and its definition
- Data dictionary, update cadence, and correction procedure

Standing requests may not be required by law; calendar repeat requests or negotiate recurring extracts.
