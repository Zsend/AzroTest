# Production Launch Checklist

This is the go/no-go standard. A polished interface is not sufficient.

## 1. Entity, governance, and brand

- [ ] Nonprofit/fiscal sponsor and any affiliated public-benefit entity approved
- [ ] Board and compensated lived-experience advisory council seated
- [ ] Conflict, donor, sponsor, correction, grievance, and publication policies adopted
- [ ] Trademark, entity-name, domain, social handle, and fundraising clearance complete
- [ ] Insurance in force: general, cyber, D&O, employment, professional/E&O as advised
- [ ] Candidate, employer, registry, privacy, public-records, cannabis-regulatory, and employment counsel assigned

## 2. Trust and legal documents

- [ ] Privacy notice, terms, accessibility statement, correction policy, and methodology finalized
- [ ] Candidate consent ledger and withdrawal workflow counsel-reviewed
- [ ] Fair Chance Employer Compact finalized
- [ ] Employer background-check boundary and FCRA posture documented
- [ ] State fair-chance and cannabis-role review completed for pilot jurisdictions
- [ ] Public-record republication, defamation/false-light, safety, and takedown protocol reviewed
- [ ] Data retention, deletion, legal hold, and subject-access workflow adopted

## 3. Security and infrastructure

- [ ] Managed Postgres, object storage, KMS, secrets, backups, and restore test
- [ ] SSO/MFA, least privilege, access review, and offboarding
- [ ] WAF, distributed rate limits, bot controls, vulnerability scanning, and alerting
- [ ] Central audit logs and incident-response runbook
- [ ] Production and staging environments isolated
- [ ] Dependency/container/SAST/secret scans clean or accepted
- [ ] External penetration test completed; critical/high findings remediated
- [ ] Accessibility audit against WCAG 2.2 AA completed
- [ ] RTO/RPO and source-outage tabletop passed
- [ ] No secrets, production records, or private documents in the source package

## 4. Registry operations

- [ ] Formal inclusion/exclusion standard adopted
- [ ] Offense and violence-screen rule set versioned
- [ ] Two independent reviewers trained and calibrated
- [ ] Source-document vault and checksum workflow operational
- [ ] Public-record request calendar, appeals, fees, and agency contacts active
- [ ] Current-custody, transfer, release, and stale-source rules tested
- [ ] Correction intake and field-freeze process tested
- [ ] First 25–100 cases privately reviewed
- [ ] Reviewer disagreement and false-inclusion audit completed
- [ ] Every public record meets the publication gate; provisional records remain private
- [ ] Coverage denominator and unavailable-data language visible

## 5. Employer demand

- [ ] At least 10 verified employers, not merely pledged logos
- [ ] At least 20 approved, currently open, paid roles
- [ ] Every role has pay, duties, location/schedule, manager, pathway, and growth plan
- [ ] Employer application and hiring policy reviewed
- [ ] Hiring managers trained
- [ ] Candidate privacy and disposition reporting tested
- [ ] Complaint/escalation contact named
- [ ] Federal Bonding referral route confirmed where applicable
- [ ] No WOTC or other incentive claim published without current legal/tax authority
- [ ] Employer status suspension/removal workflow tested

## 6. Candidate experience

- [ ] Private intake, secure access, and identity/contact verification tested
- [ ] Mobility Passport includes skills, work proof, goals, ambitions, preferences, and visibility
- [ ] Public visibility is separate, optional, and moderated
- [ ] Candidate can approve/pass each introduction and see shared fields
- [ ] Export, correction, hide, token rotation, withdrawal, and deletion tested
- [ ] Incarcerated/family/partner-assisted intake consent protocol active
- [ ] Navigator SLAs and high-need triage active
- [ ] Partners confirmed for documents, benefits, housing, transport, training, legal review, and retention
- [ ] Candidate compensation floor and good-job definition captured
- [ ] Safety and grievance escalation tested

## 7. Matching and outcomes

- [ ] Match score excludes conviction and protected/sensitive attributes
- [ ] Every suggested match has explainable reasons
- [ ] Jurisdiction/licensing review is role-specific and human-approved
- [ ] No automated adverse decision
- [ ] Interview, offer, start, wage, benefit, and retention confirmation protocol active
- [ ] 30/90/180/365-day check-ins assigned
- [ ] Advancement, credential, wage-growth, and ownership outcomes supported
- [ ] Public analytics suppress small cohorts and distinguish targets from actuals

## 8. Communications and launch

- [ ] No fabricated people, employers, jobs, starts, or impact
- [ ] Every public employer tier is evidence-based
- [ ] Every person story is opt-in, compensated where appropriate, and revocable prospectively
- [ ] Crisis, correction, press, and misinformation response owners named
- [ ] Launch language avoids defining people as “felons”
- [ ] Mission and employer obligation are clear without promising legal or employment outcomes
- [ ] Public registry and career network separation is understandable in five seconds

## Go/no-go gates

### Controlled pilot

Proceed when security basics, counsel-reviewed workflows, private data handling, ten verified employers, twenty approved roles, and trained reviewers/navigators are in place.

### Public beta

Proceed only after real candidate-consented matches, tested corrections, confirmed source freshness, no unresolved critical security issue, and an operating 24-hour high-risk escalation.

### National scale

Proceed state-by-state. A state becomes “active” only when records access, legal review, employer demand, candidate services, source monitoring, and correction capacity all meet the standard.
