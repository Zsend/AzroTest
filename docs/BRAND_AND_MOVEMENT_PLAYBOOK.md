# Justice Grows — Brand and Movement Playbook

## Brand

**Justice Grows**  
**Freedom. Work. Ownership.**

Working-name status: complete trademark, entity-name, domain, social-handle, and confusion clearance before public fundraising or national launch.

## Mission

**Repair the economic harm of cannabis prohibition by moving every eligible person from custody or conviction into freedom, dignified work, advancement, and ownership.**

## Public rallying line

**The industry should hire the people prohibition punished.**

## Vision

A cannabis economy in which legalization produces repair, people are not permanently defined by convictions, and access to work becomes a bridge to leadership and ownership.

## Strategic position

Justice Grows is not a generic job board and not a public list of “felons.” It is national mobility infrastructure combining:

- Verified, source-linked custody truth
- Candidate-owned Skills & Ambitions Passports
- Accountable employer demand
- Lawful state/role pathways
- Retention, advancement, and ownership outcomes

## Brand pillars

### Truth

Publish less, verify more. Coverage gaps and stale sources are visible. Targets never appear as outcomes.

### Agency

Candidates control visibility, introductions, story use, relocation, and withdrawal. No one must perform trauma for access.

### Mobility

The objective is not any job. It is compensation, stability, credential growth, leadership, and ownership.

### Reciprocity

Commercial cannabis benefited from conduct for which others were punished. Hiring and investment are repair obligations, not charity.

### Accountability

Employers earn status through approved roles, starts, retention, advancement, safe treatment, and complete reporting.

## Language system

Prefer:

- People with cannabis convictions
- Justice-impacted talent
- People still serving cannabis-linked commitments
- Candidate, professional, worker, founder, or leader
- “No violent offense was identified in the current commitment records reviewed as of [date].”

Avoid:

- “Felon” as a brand identity
- “Ex-con,” “offender,” or “inmate” when person-first language is available
- “Nonviolent person” as an unsupported permanent identity claim
- Mugshots, prison bars, handcuffs, distressed type, savior framing, and coerced redemption stories
- “Guaranteed placement,” “automatic eligibility,” or “clean record” claims

## Audience messages

### Candidates

Your record is not your résumé. Show your work, skills, goals, and ambition. You control who sees your profile and when an employer introduction happens.

### Employers

Do not publish a pledge. Commit paid roles, follow a lawful skills-first process, support retention, and report outcomes. Proof earns recognition.

### Cannabis industry leaders

Legalization without economic repair is incomplete. Fund the infrastructure, open roles, use supplier leverage, and build ownership pathways.

### Foundations and public systems

Justice Grows converts fragmented records and scattered services into measurable mobility: verified cases, committed jobs, candidate agency, and long-term outcomes.

### Public and media

The project documents who remains affected by cannabis prohibition while building a transparent route from punishment to economic participation.

## Campaign architecture

### Campaign 1: Founding 50 × 2

Fifty employers. Two approved roles per quarter. Public status only after evidence.

### Campaign 2: The Mobility Passport

“Your record is not your résumé.” Candidate-owned profiles centered on proof, goals, and ambition.

### Campaign 3: Every State, Every Rule

Make the 51-system barrier visible. Publish coverage and build lawful pathways state by state.

### Campaign 4: From Start to Ownership

Follow outcomes beyond placement: wage, retention, promotion, credentials, management, entrepreneurship, and equity.

## Visual direction

- Deep forest, high-energy lime, warm paper, and restrained neutral tones
- Editorial serif for moral clarity; clean sans serif for operations and data
- Growth, pathways, evidence, and human capability—not incarceration imagery
- Charts begin at zero and show source freshness, uncertainty, and coverage
- Photography, when used, is candidate-approved and focused on work, craft, leadership, and aspiration

## Proof hierarchy

1. Candidate-verified career outcome
2. Employer- and candidate-confirmed start, pay, and retention
3. Source-linked registry record with two-reviewer approval
4. Approved open role
5. Verified employer process
6. Pledge or application

Never reverse this hierarchy in communications.
