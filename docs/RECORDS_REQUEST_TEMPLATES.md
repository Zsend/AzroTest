# Public-Records and Data-Partnership Templates

These templates are operational starting points, not legal advice. Adapt each to the jurisdiction’s records law, terminology, exemptions, fee rules, and technical systems. Ask counsel to review material requests and appeals.

## 1. Generic state corrections stock extract

**Subject:** Request for electronic current-custody and commitment records involving cannabis

> Under [STATE PUBLIC RECORDS LAW], please provide an electronic extract of all adults under [AGENCY] prison custody as of [SNAPSHOT DATE] whose current commitment contains an offense involving marijuana, cannabis, tetrahydrocannabinol, hashish, or a statutory predecessor or related cannabis-specific offense.
>
> Please include the following existing fields where maintained and publicly releasable:
>
> 1. Full name and public correctional identifier.
> 2. Current custody status and status effective date.
> 3. Public facility or custody level.
> 4. Admission date and admission type.
> 5. County, court, and case number of commitment.
> 6. Every offense in the current commitment, including statute citation, statutory description, offense date, conviction date, count number, felony/misdemeanor level, and any primary, controlling, or governing-offense designation.
> 7. Sentence imposed on each count, including minimum, maximum, life term, mandatory minimum, and concurrent/consecutive structure.
> 8. Enhancements, including weapon, bodily injury, threat, gang, repeat-offender, quantity, school-zone, or other sentence enhancements.
> 9. Aggregate sentence, parole eligibility date, projected release date, maximum release date, and the dates on which those values were calculated.
> 10. Current detainers or concurrent outside-jurisdiction custody where publicly releasable.
> 11. Release action, release type, and actual release date for any person leaving custody during the requested period.
> 12. Field definitions, code tables, data dictionary, and the date/time of the extract.
>
> Please include historical and current statute versions. Where the agency cannot filter by cannabis, please provide the current-custody stock file with offense fields so the requester can apply the filter.
>
> I request the records in CSV, TSV, or another machine-readable format in which they are ordinarily maintained. I am requesting existing records or an extract from existing electronic fields, not the creation of new substantive information. Please do not convert structured data to PDF.
>
> Please describe any withheld field, the legal basis, and whether a less-redacted or aggregated alternative can be produced. Before incurring fees above $[AMOUNT], please provide an itemized estimate and discuss narrowing or an existing standard report.
>
> The request serves a noncommercial public-interest research and journalism project concerning criminal justice, incarceration, and cannabis policy. Please apply any public-interest or media fee waiver available under state law.

## 2. State admission and release delta

**Subject:** Request for monthly prison admission, commitment-change, and release delta

> Please provide a machine-readable extract for [START DATE] through [END DATE] containing:
>
> - admissions with cannabis in any current commitment offense;
> - people added to or removed from custody;
> - changes to current commitment offenses or sentence structure;
> - projected-release-date changes;
> - transfers into or out of [AGENCY] jurisdiction;
> - actual releases and release types; and
> - correctional identifier sufficient to join the delta to the prior stock extract.
>
> Please include the same offense, sentence, enhancement, and field-definition columns identified in our stock-file request dated [DATE].

Submit this monthly unless the agency offers a direct recurring feed or data-sharing agreement. Many jurisdictions do not have to honor an indefinite request for records not yet created, so operationally schedule new requests.

## 3. Federal Bureau of Prisons stock request

**Subject:** FOIA request for public current-custody records containing cannabis offenses

> Under the Freedom of Information Act, please provide a machine-readable extract of people in Bureau of Prisons custody as of [DATE] whose current federal sentence includes a marijuana/cannabis offense.
>
> Please include, where maintained and publicly releasable:
>
> - full name;
> - BOP register number;
> - current custody/location category and status effective date;
> - admission and sentence-commencement dates;
> - district, docket number, and sentencing court;
> - all current counts/statutes and offense descriptions;
> - sentence imposed by count and concurrent/consecutive structure;
> - sentence enhancements or relevant public sentencing codes;
> - aggregate sentence;
> - projected release date and calculation date;
> - release action/type and actual release date during [PERIOD]; and
> - data dictionary and field definitions.
>
> Please include cannabis-related offenses under 21 U.S.C. §§ 841, 846, 952, 960, and other relevant statutes only where the record identifies marijuana/cannabis as the controlled substance. If substance type is coded separately, please include that field and its code table.
>
> I request CSV, TSV, or another native machine-readable format. If responsive data are stored in a database, please produce an export of existing fields rather than screenshots or PDF. Please identify any record system searched, extraction date, suppressions, and exemption applied to each withheld field.

Because federal FOIA can be slow, seek a parallel research or public-information agreement rather than using FOIA as the live status layer.

## 4. California CDCR stock and release request

**Subject:** California Public Records Act request — current cannabis commitments and release actions

> Under the California Public Records Act, please provide an electronic spreadsheet containing all adults in CDCR custody as of [DATE] whose current commitment includes an offense involving marijuana or cannabis, including historical versions of Health and Safety Code sections 11357, 11358, 11359, 11360, 11361, and any other cannabis-specific offense code used in CDCR records.
>
> Please include, where maintained and publicly releasable:
>
> - full name;
> - CDCR number;
> - current custody status and public facility assignment;
> - admission date and county/court of commitment;
> - every current commitment offense, statutory code, description, and primary/controlling designation;
> - sentence and concurrent/consecutive structure by count;
> - enhancements;
> - aggregate term, parole eligibility, and projected release dates;
> - resentencing or release actions reflected in CDCR fields; and
> - field definitions and extraction timestamp.
>
> For [START DATE] through [END DATE], please separately provide actual release date, release type, and identifier for any person in the responsive cohort who left CDCR custody.
>
> Please provide CSV or Excel in the electronic format in which the data are maintained. This request seeks existing electronic records or extraction of existing fields, not the creation of new substantive information.

Do not ask CDCR to make the legal conclusion “nonviolent” for the registry. Request the complete commitment and enhancement fields, then apply the published registry standard.

## 5. Individual commitment confirmation

**Subject:** Request for public commitment and release information — [NAME / IDENTIFIER]

> Please provide the publicly releasable current commitment, sentencing, custody, and release-action information for:
>
> - Name: [FULL NAME]
> - Correctional identifier: [ID]
> - Date or year of birth, if required for matching: [PRIVATE]
>
> Specifically, please identify every offense and enhancement contributing to current custody; sentence by count; concurrent/consecutive structure; aggregate term; current custody status; projected release date; and any recent resentencing, parole, transfer, or release action. Please identify the “as of” date for each value.

Use this for unresolved records and do not publish private matching information supplied to the agency.

## 6. Data-sharing memorandum outline

A direct feed agreement should define:

1. Public-interest purpose and prohibited uses.
2. Included population and fields.
3. Full stock plus delta/event delivery.
4. Unique identifiers and field definitions.
5. Update cadence and delivery SLA.
6. Correction and backfill procedure.
7. Permitted publication and required suppressions.
8. Security, encryption, access control, and breach notice.
9. Retention and deletion.
10. Audit rights and data-quality contacts.
11. No re-identification or screening use.
12. Term, termination, and transition export.
13. Public disclosure of the agreement where lawful.
14. No agency editorial control over independently verified methodology or corrections.

## 7. Partner case-submission request

Ask advocacy organizations, attorneys, and reentry partners for:

- person’s full legal name and aliases;
- correctional identifier and system;
- court case number and jurisdiction;
- current facility/system if known;
- judgment, sentencing order, commitment record, and later modifications;
- all known current counts and enhancements;
- projected release and source;
- attorney/representative contact;
- consent choices for public narrative content; and
- whether urgent safety or privacy concerns exist.

Partner assertion alone creates a lead. Underlying records and official status are still required for confirmed publication.

## 8. Denial and appeal workflow

1. Ask the agency to identify the precise exemption and segregable portions.
2. Narrow private fields while preserving identifiers, offenses, sentence, status, and dates.
3. Ask for code tables, aggregate counts, or a standard report when row-level data are withheld.
4. Clarify that an electronic query or extraction of existing fields is requested, not a new analysis.
5. Request fee estimate and cheaper native format.
6. Appeal administratively within the deadline.
7. Coordinate parallel requests with journalists, researchers, and counsel.
8. Publish the source gap and its effect on coverage rather than inferring zero.

## 9. Tracking fields

Maintain a request log with:

- jurisdiction and agency;
- request text/version;
- submitted and due dates;
- portal/request number;
- contact;
- status;
- fees;
- scope changes;
- exemptions;
- response and appeal dates;
- files received and hashes;
- data periods;
- next recurring request date; and
- coverage impact.
