High‑level overview

The XRP Top/Bottom Detector is designed to identify cycle‑level extremes using a blended, normalized composite that is evaluated on a weekly cadence. Labels are intentionally conservative: a confirmation prints only when confidence and timing windows align.

Signal types

Label When it prints What it’s for
MAJOR TOP / MAJOR BOTTOM Weekly close Primary cycle confirmations at statistically significant extremes.
RADAR TOP / RADAR BOTTOM (optional) Weekly close Complementary confirmation track for alternative late‑phase structures.
EARLY TOP / EARLY BOTTOM Intra‑week Staging heads‑ups that can resolve at weekly close (may disappear if not confirmed).
LIGHT cues (optional) Weekly close Structure‑aware timing cues that are not part of the core confirmation logic.
WARNING / RISK context (optional, non‑XRP) Intra‑week / weekly close (varies) Market‑wide risk context projected to non‑XRP charts (not an altcoin buy/sell signal).

What “weekly confirmation” means

  • MAJOR / RADAR confirmations are designed to finalize on the weekly close.
  • EARLY heads‑ups can appear during the week and change before the close.
  • Alerts should be set on the 1‑Week timeframe to match the model’s intended scope.

Limitations and dependencies

  • Platform dependency: signals and alerts rely on TradingView market data and alert infrastructure. Outages, chart feed issues, or plan limits can affect delivery.
  • Regime shifts: structural market changes can degrade performance. No model works in all conditions.
  • No advice: outputs are educational and probabilistic; they do not constitute investment advice.

Backtests and performance statements

Any backtest or historical replay is hypothetical and has limitations (slippage, liquidity, psychology, and parameter sensitivity). Use backtests as context, not as a guarantee. The full tear‑sheet and disclosures are available in the PDF linked above.

Professional expectation: Treat confirmations as one input in a broader risk process (position sizing, stops/invalidations, and discipline). Past performance does not guarantee future results.
