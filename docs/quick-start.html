Quick‑Start Guide

Go from purchase to weekly alerts in minutes. Follow this guide to install the indicator, set the right alerts, and avoid the most common setup mistakes.

Scope & safety

  • Timeframe: this indicator is calibrated for the 1‑Week timeframe. Signals on lower timeframes are not valid.
  • Confirmations: MAJOR / RADAR confirmations finalize on the weekly close.
  • Educational only: nothing here is investment advice. Use position sizing and a written plan.

1) Get access

  1. Choose Free trial or Buy now.
  2. After checkout, submit your TradingView username (the one you will use to run the script).
  3. Access is granted via TradingView invite‑only scripts (typically within 24 hours).

2) Add the indicator in TradingView

  1. Open an XRPUSD chart (we recommend BITSTAMP:XRPUSD for long history).
  2. Switch the chart to 1W (Weekly).
  3. Open Indicators → Invite‑only Scripts and select AZRO Systems XRP Top/Bottom — Invite‑Only.
Pro tip: Keep defaults to start. The default configuration is designed to be usable immediately. Optional visual layers (LIGHT / RISK) can be explored later.

3) Recommended alert set

Set alerts on the 1‑Week chart. In the TradingView alert dialog, choose a named condition (e.g., MAJOR TOP), not “Any alert() function call”.

XRP chart (core)

Condition Frequency Purpose
MAJOR TOP Once Per Bar Close Primary weekly sell confirmation.
RADAR TOP Once Per Bar Close Complementary weekly sell confirmation (often closer to the exact top).
EARLY BOTTOM Once Per Bar Intra‑week heads‑up that a bottom may be forming (can disappear if not confirmed).
MAJOR BOTTOM Once Per Bar Close Primary weekly buy confirmation.

Non‑XRP charts (market‑wide risk layer)

If you enable the optional market‑wide layer, non‑XRP symbols can display WARNING / RISK context. These are not buy/sell confirmations for that asset; they are a risk‑on/risk‑off signal derived from the XRP model.

4) A simple, disciplined playbook

  • Use EARLY BOTTOM as a heads‑up to prepare (research, plan entries, reduce leverage), then act only when your rules say so.
  • Use MAJOR / RADAR confirmations as the “final word” at weekly close.
  • Define risk before you enter: invalidation, max loss, and how you’ll scale out if a TOP prints.

Troubleshooting

I purchased but I don’t see the script.

Invite‑only access is granted to a specific TradingView username. Confirm you submitted the correct username and are logged into that exact account. If you’re within the first 24 hours, the invite may still be processing.

I see labels on some timeframes but not others.

Signals are designed for the 1‑Week timeframe. Other timeframes can behave unpredictably and should not be used.

My alert didn’t fire.

Verify (1) the chart is on 1W, (2) the alert condition is a named condition (e.g., MAJOR TOP), and (3) TradingView alert limits have not been reached for your plan.
