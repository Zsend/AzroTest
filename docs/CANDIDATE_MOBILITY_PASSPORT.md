# Candidate Mobility Passport

## Product promise

The Mobility Passport is a candidate-owned professional identity for people whose careers have been constrained by a cannabis conviction. It lets a person present capability, evidence of work, goals, and ambition without turning conviction history into a marketplace filter.

A Passport is not a background report, legal record, public registry profile, or guarantee of employment or licensing.

## Candidate controls

Every candidate chooses one of three visibility modes:

- **Private:** visible only to the candidate and authorized Justice Grows navigators.
- **Coalition-only:** eligible for human-reviewed matching with verified employers; not public.
- **Public:** a separately consented and moderated skills profile. Contact details, exact location, custody history, conviction details, service needs, and release information are never included.

Candidates can change visibility, pause matching, reject a match, hide a public profile, rotate the secure access link, request export, correct information, or withdraw. A public profile must never be a condition of service.

## Passport contents

### Professional identity

- Candidate-selected display name or alias
- Headline and short professional statement
- Preferred role families and industries
- Location preferences, remote readiness, and opt-in relocation preferences
- Schedule, availability, transportation constraints, and compensation floor
- Languages and accessibility/accommodation preferences, collected only when necessary

### Skills and proof

- Formal employment and informal work history
- Transferable skills and tools
- Certifications, licenses, training, and union/apprenticeship experience
- Accomplishments stated as evidence: output, quality, safety, leadership, customer impact, savings, or reliability
- Candidate-approved work samples, portfolio links, references, and project evidence
- Skills normalized to O*NET occupations and competencies in production, while preserving the candidate's own language

### Goals and ambition

- Immediate stability goal
- Twelve-month work and income goal
- Three-year career ambition
- Leadership, management, licensing, entrepreneurship, or ownership objectives
- Training interests and credential plan
- Candidate-defined definition of a good job

### Support plan

The restricted service layer may track identification documents, digital access, transportation, clothing/tools, benefits, housing, legal referrals, supervision questions, training, relocation, and retention needs. These fields are excluded from public profiles and employer discovery.

## Incarcerated and recently released access

The production program needs multiple intake channels:

1. Self-service web and mobile
2. Family or advocate referral, followed by direct consent
3. Navigator-assisted phone intake
4. Paper or tablet workflow through approved correctional/reentry partners
5. Attorney or legal-aid referral
6. Pre-release cohort workshops with explicit opt-in

A referral does not create a public profile or authorize employer contact. The person must directly consent when reasonably possible.

## Matching rules

The match engine may use:

- Skills and work evidence
- Role interests
- Schedule and availability
- Location, remote, and relocation preferences
- Compensation floor
- Training readiness
- Job pathway and duties

It must not use detailed conviction information, race, ethnicity, religion, sex, gender identity, disability, medical information, immigration status, or other protected/sensitive characteristics as a match score feature. Legal or licensing eligibility is a separate, explainable, human-reviewed workflow.

Every proposed introduction must show the candidate:

- Employer and role
- Compensation and benefits
- Work location and schedule
- Why the role was suggested
- Known licensing or duty-specific questions
- What information will be shared
- The ability to approve, pass, or request help

## Public profile standard

A public profile can contain only candidate-approved professional information. Before publication, staff review for:

- Accidental contact or exact-location disclosure
- Legal-record or custody details
- Private third-party information
- Defamatory or unsafe claims
- Broken or unsafe links
- Clear, credible career content
- Separate public-profile consent

Search-engine indexing is off by default during the pilot. Candidates may later opt into discovery only after a privacy review and clear explanation of permanence and third-party copying risk.

## Career mobility ladder

The Passport should support progression rather than only an entry job:

1. **Stability:** documents, schedule, reliable income, transportation, and safe onboarding
2. **Capability:** credential, skills evidence, dependable performance, and wage growth
3. **Leadership:** lead, trainer, supervisor, manager, or specialist pathway
4. **Ownership:** profit sharing, cooperative membership, licensing, entrepreneurship, capital readiness, and mentorship

## Service levels

- Candidate intake acknowledgment: immediate
- Navigator review: two business days
- High-need triage: one business day
- Profile moderation: three business days
- Candidate-approved employer introduction: one business day after approval
- Correction or privacy escalation: same-day acknowledgment
- Deletion/withdrawal request: completed or legally explained within 30 days

## Success metrics

- Passport completion and candidate-controlled visibility mix
- Time to first viable match, interview, offer, and start
- Percentage of introductions explicitly approved by candidates
- Starts meeting candidate compensation floor
- 30/90/180/365-day retention
- Wage growth, benefits, promotion, and credential completion
- Candidate-reported safety, dignity, and agency
- Public-profile withdrawal and correction rates
- Advancement and ownership milestones
