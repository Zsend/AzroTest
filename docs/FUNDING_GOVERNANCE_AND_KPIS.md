# Funding, Governance, and KPIs

## Recommended structure

### Trust layer

A 501(c)(3) nonprofit or fiscally sponsored project controls:

- Registry methodology and publication
- Candidate data and consent
- Legal/reentry services
- Employer standards
- Impact measurement
- Corrections and grievances

### Optional services layer

A wholly owned or contractually controlled public-benefit entity may provide enterprise workflow, analytics, training, or integration services. It must not sell candidate data, charge applicants, weaken the compact, or control registry inclusion.

## Revenue mix

- Foundations and criminal-justice/reentry philanthropy
- Cannabis-industry founding sponsorships
- Workforce and training grants
- State/local cannabis-equity and community-reinvestment grants
- Employer membership or implementation fees after proof of value
- Sponsored candidate supports and relocation funds
- Research/data grants using de-identified, governed data
- Individual giving

No pay-to-remove registry records. No placement fee charged to candidates. No sale of named candidate or registry data.

## Governance

- Board with lived experience, employment, data/security, public records, cannabis regulation, and fiduciary expertise
- Compensated lived-experience advisory council
- Independent publication/correction committee
- Employer-status review committee with candidate representation
- Conflict-of-interest disclosures
- Annual methodology, security, and impact review
- Public donor and sponsor policy

## KPI hierarchy

### Trust and accuracy

- Confirmed publication precision
- Critical correction rate and time to resolution
- Source freshness compliance
- Coverage denominator transparency
- Candidate privacy incidents
- Candidate complaint resolution

### Employer demand

- Pledged, Verified, and Proven employers
- Approved roles per employer per quarter
- Interview and offer conversion
- Role withdrawal and employer no-show rate
- Median compensation and benefits
- Employer reporting completeness

### Candidate mobility

- Time to first viable match, interview, offer, and start
- Starts meeting candidate compensation floor
- 30/90/180/365-day retention
- Wage growth and promotion
- Training/credential completion
- Candidate-reported safety and dignity
- Relocation stability
- Ownership/entrepreneurship progression

### System leverage

- Cost per verified registry record
- Cost per quality-adjusted career start
- Cases per reviewer with error controls
- Public-record turnaround and appeal success
- State-rule review cycle time
- Partner service completion

## Public reporting rule

Separate:

- Actual verified results
- Pipeline activity
- Coverage estimates
- Operating targets
- Forecasts

Never blend targets or applications into placements.
