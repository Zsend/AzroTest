# Justice Grows — Mission and Brand System

## Position

Justice Grows is the national infrastructure that turns cannabis justice into durable economic mobility.

It does three things that existing advocacy, corrections, and job systems rarely combine:

1. Establishes who is still serving a cannabis-linked commitment, using reviewable evidence.
2. Builds lawful, candidate-controlled career pathways rather than publishing a stigmatizing applicant list.
3. Holds employers accountable for paid roles, compensation, retention, advancement, and ownership—not statements of support.

## Mission

**Track every person still serving a cannabis-linked commitment, support the path to freedom, and build a direct route into stable work, advancement, and ownership.**

## Vision

**An industry that repairs what prohibition broke.**

## Public promise

**The industry should hire the people prohibition punished.**

## Mobility ladder

- **Freedom:** verified custody data, legal referrals, release monitoring, and corrections.
- **Work:** skills-first matching, paid roles, fair-chance hiring, support through retention.
- **Ownership:** credentials, supervision, licensing navigation, entrepreneurship, equity access, mentorship, and capital.

## Brand principles

### Truth before scale

A smaller verified dataset is more valuable than a national-looking list built from stale locators, news stories, or incomplete convictions.

### Dignity without euphemism

Use “justice-impacted person with a cannabis conviction” or a precise custody statement. Avoid defining a human being as a “felon.” Do not use mugshots, bars, handcuffs, distressed prison typography, or coerced redemption stories.

### Reciprocity, not charity

The industry received economic permission to commercialize conduct for which people were punished. The employer message is a workforce and repair obligation, not benevolence.

### Proof over badges

Employers progress through Pledged, Verified, and Proven status. Only outcomes earn proof.

### Candidate control

No public story, identity introduction, relocation, disclosure, or employer contact without consent.

## Product architecture

- **Justice Grows Registry:** public evidence and release-monitoring layer.
- **Justice Grows Careers:** private candidate network and match workflow.
- **Fair Chance Employer Compact:** employer standards and verification.
- **State Access Engine:** jurisdiction, licensing, fair-chance, relief, and mobility routing.
- **Impact Ledger:** starts, pay, retention, advancement, complaints, and employer follow-through.
- **Ownership Pathways:** management, licensing, entrepreneurship, mentorship, and capital readiness.

## Voice

Direct, precise, ambitious, and humane. Never pitying. Never sensational. Never imply a person owes the public a story in exchange for help.

## Primary audiences

- Candidates, currently incarcerated people, and families
- Cannabis and ancillary employers
- Legal-aid and reentry organizations
- Workforce systems, unions, training providers, and community colleges
- Foundations and mission-aligned investors
- Regulators, policymakers, researchers, and journalists
