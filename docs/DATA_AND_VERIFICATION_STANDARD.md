# Data and Verification Standard

## Unit of analysis

The registry evaluates a **current correctional commitment or custody episode**. It does not label a person based on every historical charge, arrest, or conviction.

## Minimum publication test

A named public record requires:

1. Resolved identity using at least two reliable attributes.
2. Current custody or supervision status confirmed from a current official source.
3. Cannabis attribution supported by an operative judgment, commitment record, sentencing record, or equivalent primary evidence.
4. Complete review of every count, enhancement, consecutive/concurrent sentence, revocation, detainer, and resentencing action materially contributing to current custody.
5. A dated violence-screen statement.
6. Field-level source provenance and retrieval time.
7. First reviewer recommendation.
8. Independent second-review approval.
9. Freshness within the jurisdiction-specific service level.
10. Publication, privacy, and safety approval.

## Required language

Do not publish “nonviolent person” as an identity claim. Use:

> No violent offense was identified in the current commitment records reviewed as of [date].

This statement must be paired with the rule set used and an explicit note when material records remain unavailable.

## Cannabis classification

- **Cannabis-only:** every custody-producing current count is a cannabis offense.
- **Cannabis-primary:** cannabis is the principal custody driver, but other current counts exist and are disclosed to reviewers.
- **Cannabis-contributing:** cannabis contributes to custody but is not the principal driver; ordinarily not included in the public core count.
- **Historical cannabis:** cannabis history exists but does not produce current custody; excluded from the current-incarceration registry.
- **Unresolved:** records are insufficient or conflicting; private research only.

## Evidence hierarchy

1. Operative judgment, sentencing order, commitment abstract, resentencing order
2. Official corrections commitment/offense and sentence records
3. Court docket and filed charging/disposition documents
4. Official agency locator/status action
5. Attorney-provided records with authenticity review
6. Credible contemporaneous reporting
7. Family, advocate, or subject statement

Lower levels may discover a case; they do not independently establish inclusion.

## Publication states

- **Confirmed:** primary evidence supports material fields.
- **Supported:** multiple reliable sources support material fields while a primary record remains outstanding; public use only with a clear limitation.
- **Provisional:** private research queue.
- **Disputed:** public field frozen, removed, or annotated while conflicts are resolved.
- **Removed:** no longer within scope, released, erroneous, or removed for safety/privacy reasons.

## Release taxonomy

“Released” must distinguish:

- Released from the identified prison agency
- Transferred to another custodial authority
- Immigration detention or detainer transfer
- Community supervision
- Halfway house/residential reentry
- Sentence completion
- Court-ordered release
- Death
- Unknown/agency locator change

## Freshness

- Projected release within 90 days: daily or event-triggered check
- In custody, release beyond 90 days: weekly check
- Stable long sentence: monthly check plus court-alert monitoring
- Release event: same-day confirmation when possible, followed by 24–72-hour reconciliation
- Source outage: visible stale-data indicator; never silently treat as no change

## Corrections

Critical identity, custody, violence-screen, and release disputes should freeze the affected field immediately. Preserve history in the restricted audit log, conduct independent review, publish a dated correction note, and notify the affected person/representative when possible.
