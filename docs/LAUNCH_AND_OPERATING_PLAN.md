# Launch and Operating Plan

## Strategic answer

The bottleneck is not website traffic. It is reliable supply on both sides:

- **Verified people:** fragmented corrections, court, sentence, and release data.
- **Committed demand:** employers willing to submit actual paid roles and follow a defensible fair-chance process.

Run both supply chains as operations, not campaigns.

## Phase 0 — Legal, governance, and trust (weeks 0–4)

- Establish a nonprofit or fiscal-sponsor structure for the registry and candidate trust.
- Decide whether an affiliated public-benefit entity will provide employer software or services.
- Complete brand clearance.
- Retain criminal-records/public-records, employment/FCRA, privacy/security, and cannabis regulatory counsel.
- Adopt registry standard, privacy policy, corrections policy, employer compact, conflict policy, data retention, incident response, and candidate grievance process.
- Form a lived-experience advisory council with compensated members.
- Set up domain, managed secrets, email, CRM/case management, Postgres, object storage, backups, monitoring, and administrator MFA.

**Gate:** no real person is public before the publication standard and correction channel are operational.

## Phase 1 — Controlled pilot (weeks 3–12)

### Jurisdictions

- Federal custody
- California
- Idaho mobility pathway
- Four to six additional states selected by data accessibility, legal-market size, partner strength, and employer demand

### Registry

- Submit recurring public-record requests for stock files and release deltas.
- Build offense crosswalks and operative-judgment workflow.
- Privately review the first 25–100 cases.
- Measure reviewer disagreement and false-inclusion risk.
- Verify release events and post-release custody distinctions.

### Careers

- Enroll 25 founding employers.
- Approve at least 50 roles before broad candidate acquisition.
- Build a partner bench for documents, benefits, transportation, housing, training, legal review, and retention.
- Conduct 50 private candidate intakes.
- Run all first matches with human case-management review.

**Gate:** at least 10 verified employers, 20 approved jobs, counsel-reviewed workflows, and no unresolved critical security issue.

## Phase 2 — Public beta (months 4–6)

- Publish verified registry records only where standards are met.
- Launch jobs and employer status publicly.
- Start candidate-consented introductions.
- Report actual funnel metrics and data freshness.
- Publish jurisdiction coverage gaps.
- Establish a 24-hour safety/correction escalation and five-business-day standard correction target.

**Gate:** confirmed starts, candidate feedback, employer reporting, and a tested adverse-event response.

## Phase 3 — Scale (months 7–18)

- Add state pods in batches, not all at once.
- Migrate ingestion to queued connectors and structured public-record imports.
- Expand employer coalition via trade associations, social-equity programs, unions, multi-state operators, ancillary vendors, staffing firms, and supplier networks.
- Add credential wallets, training pathways, relocation grants, management tracks, entrepreneurship, and capital partners.
- Publish quarterly independent impact and accuracy reports.

## The 50 × 2 founding challenge

Recruit 50 verified employers committed to at least two approved roles per quarter. This creates up to 400 role openings a year before replacement hiring. It is a demand-creation program, not a claim that all roles will be filled.

## Employer acquisition playbook

### Priority segments

1. Social-equity and community-rooted licensees
2. Larger operators with centralized HR and measurable impact commitments
3. Ancillary software, packaging, equipment, construction, HVAC, compliance, accounting, logistics, marketing, and professional services
4. Hemp and non-intoxicating-product businesses where lawful
5. Unions, staffing firms, community colleges, and registered apprenticeship partners
6. Remote employers whose products serve the industry without plant-touching duties

### Outreach sequence

- Executive sponsor invitation centered on repair and workforce value
- 30-minute readiness assessment
- Policy/application review
- First-role workshop
- Hiring-manager training
- Candidate introduction
- Retention support
- Outcome verification and public tier

### Employer offer

- First placements free or foundation-subsidized
- Pre-screened skills and availability
- State/role pathway memo
- Federal Bonding coordination where applicable
- Onboarding and retention coach
- Verified impact reporting
- Public status earned through outcomes

## Candidate operating workflow

1. Private intake and identity/contact validation
2. Skills inventory and interest mapping
3. Immediate needs triage
4. State/role pathway review
5. Training or document gap plan
6. Match suggestions with explainable reasons
7. Candidate review and consent
8. Employer introduction
9. Interview preparation
10. Conditional-offer/licensing support
11. Start confirmation
12. 30/90/180/365-day check-ins
13. Advancement and ownership plan

## Idaho mobility pathway

Do not promise a local plant-touching job where no lawful route has been confirmed. Build a portable “mobility passport” containing:

- Skills and work history
- Identity and employment documents
- Transferable certifications
- Remote-work readiness
- Ancillary-industry role map
- Candidate-controlled relocation preferences
- Housing and transportation plan
- State-specific license review
- Legal-relief referral
- Advancement/ownership plan

## North-star metric

**Quality-adjusted 180-day career starts:** confirmed starts that meet the candidate’s compensation floor, are retained at least 180 days, and receive a candidate-reported safe-work confirmation.

Supporting metrics:

- Verified registry coverage and source freshness
- Candidate opt-in and withdrawal rates
- Employer Pledged/Verified/Proven counts
- Approved roles and employer follow-through
- Time to first interview, offer, and start
- Starting pay, benefits, wage growth, and promotion
- 30/90/180/365-day retention
- Licensing-resolution time
- Complaint and correction rates
- Candidate trust score
- Employer-specific conversion and retention

## First-year planning budget

- Executive/product/data leadership: $250k–$450k
- Case research, public records, and legal review: $200k–$500k
- Employer partnerships and career navigation: $200k–$450k
- Engineering, security, infrastructure, and data tooling: $200k–$450k
- Candidate support, training, relocation, and emergency funds: $150k–$500k
- Counsel, insurance, audit, communications, and administration: $150k–$350k

**Credible first-year range:** approximately $1.15M–$2.7M, depending on state count, internal staffing, records costs, legal intensity, and direct candidate support. Hosting is not the main cost.
