BTC Operating System glossary

What the dashboard is showing you and why it matters in live use.

Action + suggested amountThe weekly instruction: BUY, TRIM, or HOLD plus the amount tied to your plan.

This is the core output. Read the weekly instruction, size the amount, and follow the written process instead of negotiating with the market.

Market contextHigh-level cycle posture so you are not making the weekly decision blind.

The goal is not more noise. It is enough context to understand whether the system is pressing, pausing, or acting more carefully.

Modes + risk profilesDifferent policy sets for different levels of aggression and trim behavior.

Stack, Adaptive, Event, and Performance are there so the workflow can match your actual behavior and execution burden instead of forcing one policy on everyone.

Proof vs matched controlBTC-eq, x vs Auto, extra sats, and accretion keep the process accountable.

A serious product should not just tell you what to do. It should also show you how the policy is behaving versus a simple benchmark using the same cashflow assumptions.

Alerts + weekly routineWeekly Update for routine. Major Cycle Event for the rare weeks that deserve extra attention.

The alerts are there to reduce screen time, not increase it. Set them once, review on the weekly close, and keep the process calm.

Label glossary (XRP Top/Bottom Indicator)

What each label means and how it fits into the weekly XRP workflow.

MAJOR TOP / MAJOR BOTTOMCore weekly confirmations designed to anchor the biggest decisions.

Treat these as the anchor points of the cycle plan. They are close-confirmed weekly labels built to organize the biggest adds and exits.

RADAR TOP / RADAR BOTTOMSecondary confirmations that support the main workflow.

RADAR adds structure without demanding more screen time. On the top side especially, it matters because different cycles can emphasize different prints.

XRP RISK / HIGH RISKSerious top-side caution that deserves respect.

On XRP, RISK can mark the actual top or sit very near it. Treat it as an action prompt to de-risk and tighten the plan, not as background decoration.

LIGHT TOP / LIGHT BOTTOMOptional context cues for users who want more timing detail.

LIGHT is not required for the base workflow. Keep it on if you want extra context. Turn it off if you want the cleanest possible chart.

EARLY TOP / EARLY BOTTOMHeads-up staging cues that can appear and disappear before the weekly close.

EARLY is for users who intentionally want earlier but less stable information during the week. Confirmed weekly labels are the main trust layer.
