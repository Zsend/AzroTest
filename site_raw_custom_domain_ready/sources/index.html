Sources & evidence

This movement is strongest when it separates public record from testimony — and keeps both visible.

A Right For All uses three evidence layers: founder testimony, letters from inside, and public-record sources. That keeps the moral case clear without blurring what is firsthand, what is documented by people inside, and what is already visible in official systems.

Founder testimony

The founding case comes from firsthand incarceration in Idaho, including direct observation of long confinement, weak or absent free self-improvement tools, and the contradiction of tablet access without a meaningful free floor.

Letters from inside

Twenty-one handwritten letters describe the need for better educational, mental-health, and goal-setting tools. See the letter archive.

Public record

The links below document the current tablet rail, jail time data, and relevant education statements from official or primary sources.

Selected public-record sources

  • Idaho Department of Correction: Resident Communications Updates — confirms free Bridge 8 tablets for residents.
  • Jerome County Jail official page — shows GettingOut / ViaPath usage in the founding county-jail context.
  • Bureau of Justice Statistics: Jail Inmates in 2023 — national jail admissions and average time in custody.
  • Khan Academy mission — free, world-class education for anyone, anywhere.
  • Khan for Educators: out-of-school programs — lists correctional facilities as a use case.
  • Bonneville County Sheriff: GED milestone — an example showing jail-based GED work can happen, which makes uneven access even harder to excuse.

The point of the site is not to pretend nothing exists. It is to show that the baseline is still far too weak in the places where human time is being wasted most brutally — and that a stronger free floor is both morally obvious and operationally imaginable.
