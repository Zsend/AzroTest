How it works

A fair chance is not a free pass. It is a fuller evaluation.

The candidate still has to be ready. The employer still makes the decision. Prohibition Careers makes current ability easier to see and real opportunity easier to reach.

The operating path

Connect Prove Track Open
01 / Connect

Start with a real person and a real opportunity.

We look for an actual decision-maker, a defined role or paid project, clear terms, and a plausible fit. A general pledge is not an opportunity.

Candidate brings
A clear goal, honest context, readiness, and evidence of ability or serious learning.
Employer brings
Real work, clear compensation, actual requirements, and the willingness to review the person individually where lawful.
02 / Prove

Build a New Record of what the person can do now.

A criminal record can show a past event. A New Record adds the evidence that conventional screening misses.

  • Work samples and completed projects
  • References and responsibilities carried
  • Tools, skills, credentials, and problems solved
  • Evidence of reliability, judgment, and follow-through
  • A clear reason the opportunity fits
03 / Track

Count the outcome, not the activity.

A workshop is not a job. A pledge is not a hire. A start date is not retained work.

AccessIntroductions and interviews
WorkOffers, starts, pay, and hours
Stability30-, 90-, 180-, and 365-day retention
MobilityGrowth in responsibility, compensation, leadership, and ownership
04 / Open

Make verified action visible.

The long-term Open Industry system will recognize companies for what they actually do—not what they sign or say.

Employer participation, jobs created, compensation, retention, advancement, mentorship, supplier access, and ownership pathways should become measurable.

Readiness, not moral judgment

We do not decide who is a “good person.”

We assess readiness for a specific opportunity: honest communication, a clear goal, credible evidence, reliable follow-through, role fit, and lawful eligibility.

01Clear goal

The person can explain the work they are trying to reach.

02Current evidence

There is something credible to evaluate now—not only a promise.

03Follow-through

Communication, preparation, and commitments are handled reliably.

04Lawful fit

The opportunity is legally available and the real requirements can be met.

Hard boundaries

What this network does not do.

No automatic placement.

A cannabis conviction is not a credential and does not guarantee admission, contact, referral, interview, or employment.

No public conviction profile.

Candidate information stays private by default. No one must turn their past into public content to be considered.

No productive unpaid tests.

Work used by a company should be paid. Evaluation must be bounded, honest, and lawful.
