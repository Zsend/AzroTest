How it works

A fair chance needs more than a job board.

Prohibition Careers is a private connection and evidence layer. We prepare the person, verify the opportunity, make the introduction, and track what happens next.

01

Prepare

Build a New Record of current ability.

Not a rewritten criminal record. A private professional record of skills, work, references, goals, readiness, and lawful fit.

02

Connect

Reach a real decision-maker.

We match a person to an actual role, project, apprenticeship, or business mentor—not to a vague pledge or public database.

03

Prove

Let the work create the next signal.

An interview, bounded paid project, apprenticeship, or job gives both sides a responsible way to evaluate fit.

04

Grow

Measure more than a start date.

Retention, pay, responsibility, promotion, leadership, procurement, and ownership show whether opportunity became mobility.

Candidate readiness

Admission is not automatic.

A cannabis conviction is not a credential. Active introductions are reserved for people who are ready for a specific opportunity.

01Clear goal

A realistic direction and a reason the opportunity fits.

02Current evidence

Work, projects, references, credentials, experience, or a serious way to test ability.

03Honest communication

Clear facts, no inflated claims, and no hidden material constraints.

04Reliable follow-through

Responsiveness, preparation, and consistency before an introduction is made.

05Lawful fit

The role, jurisdiction, licensing rules, and individual circumstances must align.

Employer standard

Bring something real.

A role must have clear work, compensation, requirements, location, timing, and any genuine legal or licensing restrictions.

01

Paid work

No productive unpaid tests, candidate-paid programs, or vague “entrepreneur” offers.

02

Clear terms

State the wage or project fee, expected hours, scope, location, and timeline.

03

Individual review

Where lawful, evaluate the nature of the role, the record, time passed, and current evidence.

04

Room to grow

Do not make entry-level placement a permanent ceiling when performance earns more.

Pilot model

Small enough to run now. Structured to scale later.

One inbox. One private intake. A few prepared candidates. A few real opportunities. Verified outcomes before public claims.
