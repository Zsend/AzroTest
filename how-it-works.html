XRP Top/Bottom Detector • v1.0.9

How it works

Built to read cycle extremes on the weekly chart, with an emphasis on clean confirmations. This page explains signals, setup, alerts, and the operating principles that keep the tool practical.

Quick setup Signal glossary Alerts Toggles Limitations

Quick setup

A clean setup is repeatable. This keeps you from “tuning” signals emotionally.

1) Load on weekly XRPUSD

The detector is designed for the 1W chart. Apply it to an XRPUSD market you trust. Confirmations evaluate on weekly close.

If you switch exchanges, treat that as a new dataset and re‑validate.

2) Set your display toggles

v1.0.9 includes: label distance spacing, light‑mode palette, a soft‑confirm filter for EARLY labels, and optional risk‑colored price line.

Recommended baseline

  • RADAR TOP: on (default).
  • RADAR BOTTOM: off (unless you explicitly want that alternate track).
  • Soft‑confirm filter: on (reduces “blip” EARLY prints).
  • Label distance: use spacing to prevent overlaps on mobile.

Signal glossary

Confirmations evaluate on the weekly bar close. “During the week” marks are staging cues, not confirmations.

MAJOR TOP / MAJOR BOTTOM

The primary cycle confirmations. They print only when multiple layers align. Use these for high‑conviction “cycle extreme” moments — not for frequent trading.

Tip: Treat MAJOR as a decision point for your pre‑written plan (scale out / scale in, rebalance, etc.).

RADAR TOP (default) / RADAR BOTTOM (optional)

A parallel confirmation track designed to capture late‑phase structures that may not meet MAJOR’s path. RADAR TOP is enabled by default. RADAR BOTTOM is optional (off by default).

RADAR is still weekly‑close based. Don’t treat it as a rapid “ping.”

EARLY TOP / EARLY BOTTOM

Transient staging marks that can appear during the week and may disappear before the weekly close. Think of EARLY as “conditions developing” — not a final signal.

EARLY can help you pay attention; confirmations are evaluated at the 1W close.

LIGHT TOP / LIGHT BOTTOM

Lightweight timing cues. They are intentionally non‑authoritative — a heads‑up that structure may be shifting. Use LIGHT to tighten attention and review your plan.

RISK overlays

Risk triangles highlight caution windows. They are independent of confirmation signals. In v1.0.9, XRP risk triangles are always shown; non‑XRP risk triangles can be enabled.

Risk overlays are context. They don’t replace MAJOR/RADAR confirmation logic.

Alerts

Set alerts once. Then let the system work — without staring at the chart.

Core alerts

  • MAJOR TOP
  • MAJOR BOTTOM

These are the high‑signal, low‑frequency alerts.

Optional alerts

  • RADAR TOP (default track)
  • RADAR BOTTOM (if enabled)
  • RISK (if you want caution notifications)
Operational principle
Alerts should send you to a plan, not to a Twitter feed. If you don’t have rules for entries/exits/position sizing, write them before relying on signals.

Toggles and modes (v1.0.9)

Quality‑of‑life controls designed to improve readability and reduce false attention triggers.

Label spacing

Increase label distance to avoid overlap in compressed mobile viewports.

Light‑mode palette

Alternate colors tuned for white backgrounds and high‑contrast displays.

Soft‑confirm filter

Reduces transient EARLY prints by requiring stronger timing alignment.

Risk‑colored price line

Optional overlay that colors the price line based on risk context (informational).

Non‑XRP risk triangles

Optional. v1.0.9 sunsets “WARNING” labels on non‑XRP charts, but allows risk triangles if enabled.

Limitations (read this)

Indicators reduce noise; they don’t remove uncertainty. Treat the tool as one input, not authority.

Weekly close only

Confirmations are evaluated on the weekly close. Intra‑week volatility can be large. If you need precise entry points, your execution layer must handle that.

Regime shifts happen

Backtests can break. Liquidity, market structure, and participant behavior change. Always size positions so you can survive being wrong.
