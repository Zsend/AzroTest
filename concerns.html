Concerns, addressed

Expand each item for a concise response. If you need deeper detail, use the full concerns PDF.

Quantum computing
Not an immediate threat. Changes should happen only when necessary and with broad technical consensus. Don’t rush upgrades based on headlines.
Power grid / internet outages
If infrastructure fails, all digital systems fail. Bitcoin is decentralized and can resume when connectivity returns; redundancy improves resilience.
“Backed by nothing”
Backed by its properties: provable scarcity, durability, portability, decentralization, and proof‑of‑work security. It behaves like digital property, not a promise from an issuer.
Energy use / ESG backlash
Energy is the cost of incorruptible security. Mining tends toward the lowest-cost energy and can use curtailed or stranded power; it can also support grid economics where designed well.
51% attack / mining centralization / censorship
Attacks are extraordinarily expensive and self‑defeating. Incentives push miners to secure the network because network integrity is what gives mined coins value.
Protocol bugs / governance / forks
Stability is a feature. Bitcoin changes slowly by design; governance is conservative because “fast fixes” can create new risks.
Volatility / drawdowns
Deep drawdowns are normal for an emerging monetary asset. A long horizon and a sustainable allocation are the primary controls; volatility may compress as adoption grows.
Regulatory risk / bans / taxes
Regimes vary. The trend in many markets is toward clearer rules and institutional integration, but policy can change. Treat compliance as part of the operating system.
Custody risk (lost keys, hacks, exchange failure)
Bitcoin rewards responsibility. Use strong operational security, consider multi‑sig, and choose custody solutions appropriate to your balance sheet and expertise.
Privacy / surveillance / fungibility
Bitcoin is not inherently anonymous. Use good hygiene and consider privacy practices that fit your risk profile while respecting applicable laws.
Scalability / fees / UX limits
Layer 1 is a high‑security settlement layer. Everyday payments and higher throughput typically live on layers and rails built on top.
Whales / manipulation / leverage cascades
Markets mature as liquidity and participation grow. Avoid leverage; a disciplined plan reduces the need to react to short-term cascades.
Competition (“better crypto” replaces it)
Bitcoin’s primary advantage is decentralization and issuerlessness. Many alternatives are ventures or platforms with different risk profiles and governance dynamics.

Risk disclosure

Bitcoin is volatile, and treasury policies can fail if they are under‑funded, under‑governed, or paired with leverage. Use a sustainable allocation, maintain buffers, and align with your tax/legal advisors.
