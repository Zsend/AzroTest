Families should not have to compete with learning

A typed excerpt for readability, paired with the original handwritten letter.

Selected excerpt from Jose C. · Jerome County Jail · Idaho

Jose writes from the Jerome County Jail that every day feels the same. He says there is no education, books cannot be sent in, and basic learning that should be available still ends up feeling expensive or out of reach on the tablet.

He has a young child and girlfriend in Idaho and says that when money is limited, he would rather use what he has to stay connected to his family than spend it trying to access learning content.

His letter makes the trade-off visible in human terms: families should not have to compete with education, and people inside should not have to choose between seeing their child and trying to better themselves.
