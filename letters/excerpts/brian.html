When the free section runs out, the mind starts to slip

This page presents a typed excerpt, lightly edited for readability and formatting. The original handwritten letter is linked alongside it.

Selected excerpt from Brian W. · County jail letter

Brian describes scrolling through the free section of a jail tablet as an indigent inmate with very little money. He noticed educational content in the pay-per-minute area and immediately recognized that it could have helped him stay productive and protect his mental health.

Instead, after roughly sixty days, he says he was already struggling mentally. He asked for help, but the support he expected did not produce much change, leaving him once again with nothing on the tablet that could keep him engaged in a healthy direction.

His point is direct: more tools on the free side of the device could have helped his mental health and overall well-being during custody.
