Without real opportunities, people fall back on what is easy

A typed excerpt for readability, paired with the original handwritten letter.

Selected excerpt from Wes · County jail letter

Wes writes that from age twenty-one to twenty-five he spent most of his time locked up. He says that across the county jails he has been in, educational classes were effectively absent. He was eventually able to earn a GED during retained jurisdiction, but there was nothing meaningful beyond that.

He connects the lack of access directly to repeat failure. People his age on the outside get chances to try different things, build skill sets, and discover career paths. When those chances never arrive, he says, it becomes easier to fall back on what is familiar even when it leads in the wrong direction.

His letter reframes the problem as a skills problem, not just a punishment problem. Real learning opportunities would give people a better chance to progress, grow, and succeed after release.
