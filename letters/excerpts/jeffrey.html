This time could be used to retrain the brain

A typed excerpt for readability, paired with the original handwritten letter.

Selected excerpt from Jeffrey H. · Jerome County Jail · Idaho

Jeffrey Hill, fifty-four years old, writes that he has spent at least twenty years of his life incarcerated because of addiction. In his view, the jail environment still offers only minimal access to education and rehab, and even what little learning exists on the tablet often comes with a charge.

He says institutional education should be free. Instead, people are pushed through harsh sentences and unproductive conditions with a free Bible but no meaningful free learning path.

His closing line captures the entire case in plain language: the time is already being taken, the device is already there, and that time could be used to help a person rebuild instead of decay.
