Free in the regular world, paywalled in jail

A typed excerpt for readability, paired with the original handwritten letter.

Selected excerpt from James S. · County jail letter

James writes about the lack of programs in county jail and makes a point that is hard to ignore: the same educational app that is free in the regular world can still sit behind a paywall inside jail.

He does not pretend that everyone who comes to jail wants to better themselves. His point is that the people who actually do want to change should not lose their shot simply because they do not have money on their books.

He also notes that in-person classes are often limited by population, staffing, or eligibility rules. A stronger free tablet baseline would not solve everything, but it would give more people a real chance to learn when the classroom path is blocked.
