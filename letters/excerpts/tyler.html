Learning should not be a luxury inside

A typed excerpt for readability, paired with the original handwritten letter.

Selected excerpt from Tyler W. · Jerome County Jail · Idaho

The founder’s legal name is removed from the public copy by request; the testimony is otherwise preserved.

I was incarcerated in the Jerome County Detention Center in Jerome, Idaho. I was on a state hold and facing a federal case. I had not experienced federal prison, but I had spent more than twelve years in the Idaho state prison system, where I had access to only a small number of vocational classes and even fewer real opportunities to educate myself.

I have an extremely active mind. I’m always thinking about new ideas, different theories, and bigger questions about the world. One day I found a section of Khan Academy on the tablet that was full of things I actually wanted to learn. It felt amazing to have that kind of knowledge sitting right there in front of me.

The problem is that we are charged to use it, and I do not have money like that. I love to keep learning. I just wish it were free. It feels unfair that something built to teach people is still out of reach for the people inside who are hungry to learn.
