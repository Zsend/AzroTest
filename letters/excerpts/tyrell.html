Reform is impossible without real tools

A typed excerpt for readability, paired with the original handwritten letter.

Selected excerpt from Tyrell M. · Jerome County Jail · Idaho

Tyrell McWaters writes that while he was incarcerated in Jerome County Jail on a drug possession charge, he started to see the same pattern everywhere around him. On the outside, pain and poverty pushed people toward substances they could not afford to replace safely. Inside, he says, the same scarcity shows up again as a lack of affordable knowledge and education.

He argues that when what little learning exists is too expensive, people drift back toward what is already available around them, and the system ends up training the very patterns it claims to correct.

His conclusion is blunt and useful: if society genuinely wants rehabilitation and safe reentry, it has to provide the tools that make rehabilitation possible. Otherwise people will keep coming back to the same place with the same missing foundation.
