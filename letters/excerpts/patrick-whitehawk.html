County jail teaches the wrong lessons when nothing better is offered

This page presents a typed excerpt, lightly edited for readability and formatting. The original handwritten letter is linked alongside it.

Selected excerpt from Patrick W. · Jerome County Jail · Idaho

Patrick White Hawk Jr. writes that he has spent much of his adult life cycling through Jerome County Jail. He already earned a GED in prison, but he says most of his time was actually spent in county jail, where the daily environment taught the wrong lessons: bad influences, drugs, fighting, crime stories, and better ways to keep repeating the same mistakes.

His letter argues that county jail would be a very different place if people had educational tools that helped them change their thinking, learn a trade, and build a different direction for their lives.

He puts the issue in family terms. If people had better access to education or job training earlier, he believes the cycle of incarceration could have been far less destructive for both them and the people who depend on them.
