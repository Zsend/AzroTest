When law blocks the door

Employer willingness cannot override licensing law.

Some jurisdictions limit or prohibit direct cannabis-industry work based on conviction history. Those barriers must be identified honestly.

When direct placement is not lawful, opportunity can still exist through cannabis-adjacent companies, technology, design, packaging, logistics, professional services, remote work, mentorship, procurement, entrepreneurship, and policy reform.

Accountability

Recognition should follow verified action.

The future Open Industry standard will not reward a logo, statement, or pledge. It will measure real opportunities, hires, pay, retention, advancement, mentorship, supplier access, and ownership pathways.
