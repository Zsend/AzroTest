# Justice Grows brand and mission

## Brand

**Name:** Justice Grows  
**Tagline:** Proof over promises.  
**Category:** The proof layer for cannabis justice.  
**Core line:** Legalization created an industry. Justice must create a future.

## Mission

Connect people harmed by cannabis felony convictions to durable economic mobility, and make the cannabis industry publicly accountable for verified roles, starts, retention, advancement, equity, and ownership.

## Brand promise

Justice Grows does not count logos, donations, pledges, conference panels, marketing campaigns, or vague “impact.” It counts evidence-backed opportunities and candidate-confirmed outcomes.

## The problem

Cannabis companies can benefit from the cultural and commercial normalization of cannabis while people punished under prohibition remain excluded from work, housing, capital, licensing, and social legitimacy. Advocacy without measurable mobility can turn affected people into symbols instead of participants in the industry’s upside.

## The solution

Justice Grows creates one connected system:

1. Candidates own a private professional identity centered on skills, work, goals, ambition, and terms.
2. Employers submit real paid roles and accept a documented fair-chance standard.
3. Candidates approve every introduction.
4. Starts, retention, advancement, equity, and ownership are separately verified.
5. Employers earn public credit only for the highest completed proof stage.
6. Public industry claims can be reviewed neutrally, with evidence and right of reply.
7. Cannabis-custody records are published only after strict source and reviewer gates.

## Audience order

1. People directly affected by cannabis felony convictions
2. Employers with real paid roles and executive ownership
3. Legal, reentry, training, workforce, and capital partners
4. Journalists, researchers, investors, regulators, and the public

## Voice

- Direct, calm, dignified, and evidence-led
- Urgent without being inflammatory
- Specific about what is known, unknown, verified, and targeted
- Candidate-first; never pity-based
- Professional enough for employers, rigorous enough for journalists, clear enough for families

## Language standard

Use **“people with cannabis felony convictions”** or **“people harmed by cannabis prohibition.”** Do not reduce people to “felons.”

For the registry, use:

> No violent offense was identified in the current commitment records reviewed as of [date].

Do not label a person inherently “nonviolent.” The claim is about the records reviewed and the current commitment, not a permanent identity or character judgment.

## Story rights

A job application never grants an employer, sponsor, partner, or Justice Grows marketing rights. Public use of a person’s identity, image, story, or conviction history requires a separate, specific, informed agreement that defines use, duration, withdrawal, and fair compensation.

## Commercial independence

Donations, sponsorships, memberships, partnerships, and advertising cannot alter proof levels, rankings, methodology, review speed, corrections, findings, or private candidate access.
