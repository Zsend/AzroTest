What problem this solves

Most businesses protect cash for bills, but not necessarily for reserve.

Once the month is covered, surplus cash still needs a job. Some of it should stay liquid. Some of it should stay ready. Only one bucket is truly long-run reserve.

Reserve Standard is designed to separate those jobs, preserve operating flexibility, and make reserve construction simpler to run.

The four cash jobs

Not every saved dollar should live by the same rule.

Operating cash

Keeps payroll, vendors, taxes, rent, and working capital moving.

Safety cash

Buys the company time when revenue softens or something breaks.

Opportunity cash

Stays ready for obvious, high-conviction moves inside the business.

Reserve cash

Should carry stored business energy forward instead of quietly weakening.

The key separation: operating cash is about access. Reserve cash is about long-run strength.

Why Bitcoin can fit reserve

The reserve job needs scarcity, direct ownership, and digital liquidity.

Why familiar defaults fall short

  • Cash is necessary for immediate obligations, but weak for long-duration reserve.
  • Equities and funds add valuation, management, and dilution risk.
  • Real estate and gold preserve scarcity, but add friction, custody burden, and slower settlement.

Why Bitcoin matters here

  • Fixed supply against a denominator that keeps weakening.
  • Direct ownership without a board, tenant, banker, or committee in the middle.
  • Portability, divisibility, liquidity, and verifiable scarcity for a digital operating world.

FAQ

Plain-English answers.

Is this trading?

No. Reserve Standard is a reserve-building system with optional liquidity tools inside written guardrails, not a leverage-driven trading workflow.

Is this an all-in strategy?

No. The design assumes protected operating cash first, preserved opportunity capital, conservative sizing, and a real buffer.

Why not just buy Bitcoin blindly every week?

Because the business still has to protect operations, preserve ready cash, and keep a process that survives stress and volatility.

What happens after the Reserve Review?

If there is fit, the next step is implementation: policy, liquidity guardrails, workflow setup, and an operating cadence the company can actually keep.
