For candidates

Show the work a background check cannot see.

A conviction does not guarantee an opportunity. It should not make everything else about you disappear.

We help capable people organize current evidence, reach real decision-makers, and compete for paid work.

What counts

Your current record of effort and ability.

You do not need a perfect resume. You do need a clear, honest way to show what you can contribute now.

Work

Jobs, contracts, physical work, operations, service, or responsibilities carried consistently.

Projects

Products, systems, designs, research, code, art, business attempts, or problems solved.

References

People who can speak to reliability, learning, judgment, leadership, or quality.

Growth

Credentials, training, changed habits, sustained effort, and evidence that you finish what you start.

What we ask

Be ready to earn the opportunity.

No one is introduced only because they have a cannabis conviction.

Clarity

Know what you are trying to do.

Choose a realistic direction rather than asking an employer to invent your career.

Evidence

Give the employer something real to evaluate.

A work sample, project, reference, result, or a responsible paid test of ability.

Follow-through

Build trust before the introduction.

Respond, prepare, show up, and be honest about constraints.

Your dignity stays yours

No public profile. No forced story.

Your conviction will not be displayed in a public talent database. You will not be required to appear in fundraising, press, social media, or testimonials.

Candidate access is free. Joining does not guarantee contact, referral, interview, or employment.
