Evidence, not hype

The work is moral. The claims stay disciplined.

A Right For All separates what is established, what has been observed firsthand, and what still needs to be tested.

The method

Testimony shows why. Research supports broader claims. Open questions stay open.

1

Established evidence

Peer-reviewed research, official statistics, inspection findings, and public records.

2

Firsthand evidence

Founder experience, letters, photographs, and direct accounts showing lived reality.

3

Open questions

Promising ideas that require professional review or real-world testing before certainty.

Claims discipline

What the site will and will not say.

We will say

  • Correctional tablets already exist in many settings.
  • Meaningful free learning remains uneven and can be paywalled.
  • Correctional education is an evidence-backed first step.
  • Neurodivergent people in custody can be unidentified or unsupported.
  • The first demand is free GED prep and basic wellness.

We will not claim without proof

  • That every facility has the same conditions.
  • That one combined suicide-risk multiplier is settled.
  • That the full application is built or deployed.
  • That a pilot, partner, vendor, facility, or funder has committed.
  • That software replaces educators, clinicians, attorneys, or human relationships.
