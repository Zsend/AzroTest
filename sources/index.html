Evidence, not hype

The work is moral. The claims stay disciplined.

A Right For All separates established evidence, field evidence, and open hypotheses. That is how a movement stays powerful without pretending every answer is settled.

The method

Testimony shows why. Research supports system claims. Open questions stay open.

Established evidence

Peer-reviewed research, official statistics, and public records supporting system-level claims.

Field evidence

Founder testimony, letters, photographs, and current-state observations showing lived reality.

Open hypotheses

Promising ideas—such as specific product effects or compounded risk—that require testing before public certainty.

Claims discipline

What the site will and will not do.

We will say

  • The device and distribution rail already exist in many settings.
  • Access to meaningful free learning remains uneven and can be paywalled.
  • Correctional education is a strong evidence-backed wedge.
  • Neurodivergent people in custody can be unidentified and unsupported.
  • The first demand is free GED prep and basic wellness.

We will not claim without proof

  • That every facility has the same conditions.
  • That one combined suicide-risk multiplier is scientifically settled.
  • That the full app is built, approved, integrated, or deployed.
  • That a pilot, partner, vendor, funder, or facility has committed.
  • That AI replaces educators, clinicians, attorneys, or human judgment.
