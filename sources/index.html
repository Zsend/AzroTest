Sources & evidence

The work is moral, but the claims have to stay disciplined.

A Right For All separates firsthand evidence, letters from inside, public-record sources, professional review, and open hypotheses. That is how a movement stays powerful without drifting into hype.

Founder testimony

The founding case comes from firsthand incarceration in Idaho, including direct observation of long confinement, absent books, weak or absent free self-improvement tools, and the contradiction of tablet access without a meaningful free floor.

Letters from inside

Twenty-one handwritten letters describe the need for better educational, mental-health, and goal-setting tools. See the letter archive.
