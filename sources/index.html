Sources & evidence

This movement is strongest when it keeps testimony, letters, and public record visible at the same time.

A Right For All uses three evidence layers: founder testimony, letters from inside, and public-record sources. That keeps the moral case clear without blurring what is firsthand, what is described by people inside, and what is already visible in official systems.

Founder testimony

The founding case comes from firsthand incarceration in Idaho, including direct observation of long confinement, absent books, weak or absent free self-improvement tools, and the contradiction of tablet access without a meaningful free floor.

Letters from inside

Twenty-one handwritten letters describe the need for better educational, mental-health, and goal-setting tools. See the letter archive.
