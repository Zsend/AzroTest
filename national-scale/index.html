National scale · 100-year institution

Build the standard, not just the campaign.

A Right For All should become the institution that defines, protects, measures, funds, and improves the free education and wellness floor on correctional tablets.

The incarcerated-user layer remains free forever. The sustainable model funds the infrastructure around that free floor through agencies, vendors, implementation, certification, training, research, philanthropy, and reserve/endowment capital.

Long-term mission lock

No prisoner or family should have to pay for the baseline human-development floor.

  • Free GED prep and basic wellness remain the first demand.
  • Procurement language becomes the control point.
  • Standards, certification, evidence, and reserve capital become the moat.

Future-back roadmap

From evidence-preserved site to permanent institution.

The project should move through professional review, public-safe launch, pilot/exploration, repeatable implementation, state expansion, certification, annual reporting, recurring revenue, and eventually endowment-backed permanence.

Short termFinish legal/provenance review, professional review, public launch gate, and first partner path.
Medium termBuild Free Floor Standard v1.0, implementation playbook, procurement clause, and first pilot/exploration evidence.
Long termDevelop certification, national scorecards, recurring institutional revenue, governance mission lock, and reserve/endowment.

Permanent moat

The moat compounds across trust, standards, data, and capital.

Short-term moat

Truth and discipline.

Founder-attested evidence, clear claims boundaries, free-user doctrine, and professional credibility.

Medium-term moat

Standard and procurement.

Free Floor Standard, contract language, implementation playbooks, and trusted advisor network.

Long-term moat

Institution and reserve.

Certification, scorecards, evidence memory, recurring revenue, governance, and endowment-backed mission protection.
