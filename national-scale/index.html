Home→The long game
National scale · 100-year institution

Build the standard, not just the campaign.

The first demand is a required Free Floor on correctional tablets. The full potential is a permanent institution that keeps the baseline free, carries progress from intake to home, and reaches vulnerable people earlier.

Long-term mission lock

Guarantee the floor. Keep the path alive. Find the minds we miss.

Every horizon reinforces the same principle: human potential is not disposable.

Movement flywheel

Witness. Trust. Talent. Standard. Pilot. Proof. Adoption. Permanence.

Witness

Letters and firsthand evidence make the problem real.

Trust

Clear claims and professional review make the work credible.

Talent

Experts and unusual builders add capability.

Standard

The Free Floor becomes specific, measurable, and required.

Pilot

A thin, useful implementation tests the model.

Proof

Real-world learning improves the standard and the case.

Adoption

Law, purchasing, policy, partnerships, and integrations expand access.

Permanence

Governance and long-term capital keep the baseline free.

Time horizons

Every horizon has a different job.

Now–12 months

Become credible and implementation-ready.

  • Launch the evidence-led movement
  • Publish Free Floor Standard v0.1
  • Build the founding advisor network
  • Prototype the simple daily workbook
  • Identify one credible Idaho path
1–3 years

Prove continuity from custody to community.

  • Run a first implementation
  • Carry progress across transfers
  • Test the living Success Map
  • Create warm resource matches
  • Turn learning into Standard v1.0
3–10 years

Make the Free Floor a national expectation.

  • Expand across facilities and states
  • Place the standard in law and contracts
  • Operate The Bridge across custody and reentry
  • Build The Minds We Miss upstream
  • Establish recurring revenue and reserves
The moat

Trust, standards, data, relationships, and capital reinforce one another.

Short term

Truth and disciplined judgment

Rights-cleared evidence, clear claims, free-access doctrine, and professional credibility.

Medium term

Standard and implementation memory

Policy language, contracts, playbooks, trusted advisors, technical integrations, and real-world learning.

Long term

Institutional legitimacy and reserve

Certification, reporting, longitudinal evidence, recurring revenue, governance, and endowment-backed independence.

North-star metric

How many people have meaningful, no-cost access to the Free Floor?

Supporting measures can include facilities covered, free GED access, wellness access, accessibility, use, completion, release planning, warm handoffs, user-reported usefulness, privacy incidents, and implementation quality.

The century goal

A permanent national institution that defines, protects, measures, funds, and improves the free human-development minimum inside incarceration and across reentry—without depending on one founder, vendor, party, or funding cycle.
