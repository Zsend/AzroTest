Home→The long game
National scale · 100-year institution

Build the standard, not just the campaign.

The first demand is a free floor on correctional tablets. The full potential is a permanent institution that defines, protects, measures, funds, and improves human-development access in custody and across reentry.

Long-term mission lock

No prisoner or family should pay for the baseline.

  • Free GED prep and basic wellness remain the first demand.
  • Procurement language becomes the control point.
  • Standards, evidence, implementation memory, and reserve capital become the moat.
The movement flywheel

Witness → Trust → Talent → Standard → Pilot → Proof → Adoption → Permanence

Witness

Letters and firsthand evidence make the problem real.

Trust

Disciplined claims and professional review make the project credible.

Talent

Experts and unusual builders add capability.

Standard

The free floor becomes measurable and contractable.

Pilot

A thin serious implementation tests the model.

Proof

Real evidence improves the standard and the case.

Adoption

Procurement, partnerships, policy, and integrations expand access.

Permanence

Governance and reserve capital keep the user layer free.

Time horizons

Every horizon has a different job.

Now–12 months

Become credible and pilot-ready.

  • Launch the evidence-led site
  • Publish Free Floor Standard v0.1
  • Build the founding advisor circle
  • Define The Minds We Miss agenda
  • Identify one credible implementation route
1–3 years

Prove a repeatable model.

  • Run a first implementation
  • Validate accessibility and privacy
  • Build reentry continuity
  • Document procurement and support
  • Turn learning into Standard v1.0
3–10 years

Become a national expectation.

  • Expand across states and facility types
  • Place the free floor in contracts and policy
  • Certify vendors and facilities
  • Publish annual access scorecards
  • Build recurring institutional revenue
The moat

Trust, standards, data, relationships, and capital reinforce one another.

Short term

Truth and disciplined judgment

Rights-cleared evidence, clear claims boundaries, free-access doctrine, and professional credibility.

Medium term

Standard and implementation memory

Procurement language, playbooks, trusted advisors, technical integrations, and real-world learning.

Long term

Institutional legitimacy and reserve

Certification, scorecards, longitudinal evidence, recurring revenue, governance, and endowment-backed independence.

North-star metric

How many people have meaningful, no-cost access to the Free Floor?

Supporting measures include facilities covered, GED access, basic-wellness access, accessibility, usage, completion, reentry planning, user-reported usefulness, implementation quality, privacy incidents, and institutional cost effects.
