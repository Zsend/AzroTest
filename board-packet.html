Non-negotiables
  • No leverage. No margin, borrowing, derivatives, lending, rehypothecation, or yield products.

  • Rules over emotion. No discretionary trading; exceptions are documented.

2) Roles & controls

Treasury programs reduce risk by separating approvals, execution, and reporting.

Owner / Board
Approves policy, budgets, and major changes.
Treasury lead
Runs the monthly process, produces reports, ensures compliance.
Executor
Executes buys/sells per policy; cannot self-approve non-routine actions.
Control owner (optional)
Independent review and sign-off on monthly report and exception log.
Retention & audit trail

Retain trade confirmations, custody statements/addresses, policy exceptions (reason + approvals), and monthly reports in internal systems of record.

3) Operating cadence

Weekly
Allocate operating surplus per policy; execute buys; retain confirmations.
Monthly
Publish KPI report (BTC, BTC-eq, buffer runway, baseline comparison, exceptions); review and sign off.
Quarterly
Reaffirm policy sizing (budget + buffer) and roles; evaluate custody/control updates.

4) Reporting KPI set

Use the same definitions every month. This keeps reviews consistent and avoids “moving goalposts.”

BTC balance
BTC held in custody (retain statements/addresses).
BTC-eq
BTC + (remaining cash buffer ÷ spot BTC). Comparable reserve KPI.
Buffer runway
Cash buffer ÷ monthly operating expenses (optional, board-friendly).
Baseline
Auto-buy using the same dollars and schedule — the control group.
Sats accretion
(Strategy BTC − baseline BTC) in satoshis; compounding advantage.
Exceptions
Any trim, custody change, incident, or policy override (logged with rationale + approvals).
